## 2. How to Contest — Step by Step

### Step 1: Plead Not Guilty (30-DAY DEADLINE)

- Check "not guilty" on the reply coupon attached to the ticket
- Sign and mail to the address on the coupon, OR file in person at the Bureau des infractions et amendes
- **Do NOT pay the fine** — payment = guilty plea = points recorded immediately

### Step 2: Request Disclosure (same time as plea)

Write on the reply form: **"I request the disclosure of the evidence / Je demande la communication de la preuve."**

This entitles you to receive:
- Officer's notes (carnet de notes)
- Radar/lidar device make, model, serial number
- Calibration/verification records for the device on that date
- Officer's training certificate for the device

**Do NOT explain your defense on the form — it goes to the prosecutor.**

### Step 3: Wait for Hearing Notice (6-12 months — normal)

### Step 4: Review Disclosure Package

When received, check:
- Are calibration records present and dated to the day of your ticket?
- Do officer's notes describe YOUR vehicle specifically at the moment of the reading?
- Are nighttime conditions and traffic volume noted?
- Is the officer's qualification for that specific device documented?

### Step 5: Pre-Trial Negotiation (lawyer handles this)

Before the hearing, your lawyer contacts the prosecutor to negotiate:
- Speed reduction to a lower bracket (fewer demerit points)
- Alternative charge with fewer or no demerit points
- Withdrawal if evidence is deficient

### Step 6: Hearing (if no deal)

At the Cour du Québec (SQ tickets go to provincial court, not municipal court).

## 3. Your Defenses — Ranked

### Defense 1 (STRONGEST): Vehicle Identification at Night

**The prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt that YOUR vehicle — not another vehicle nearby — was the one measured.**

Cross-examination points:
- How many vehicles were on the road at that moment?
- Was the officer stationary or moving?
- At what distance was the reading taken?
- How did the officer confirm YOUR specific vehicle triggered the reading?
- Does the device distinguish between vehicles in multiple lanes?
- Can the officer describe your vehicle by make, model, color as noted at the time of the reading (not at the stop)?

Radar can pick up multiple vehicles simultaneously. Lidar requires precise aiming — at night, at highway speeds, with other vehicles, this precision is challengeable.

### Defense 2 (STRONG): Calibration Gaps

The prosecution must prove:
1. Officer is a qualified operator for that specific device
2. Device was tested before AND after the shift
3. Tests confirmed accurate functioning

If calibration records are missing or incomplete = device reliability is unproven = speed measurement has no evidentiary foundation.

### Defense 3: Burden of Proof

Prosecution must prove ALL elements beyond reasonable doubt:
1. Your identity as the driver
2. Your specific vehicle was measured
3. The speed recorded
4. The posted speed limit
5. Device reliability

Doubt on ANY one element = acquittal.

### Defense 4: Disclosure Failures

If prosecution doesn't provide complete disclosure:
- Request adjournment at hearing
- If still incomplete, argue trial fairness compromised (CPP arts. 139, 146)
- Bring your written disclosure request as proof you asked

### Defense 5 (FALLBACK): Negotiated Reduction

Even without a technical win, a lawyer can often negotiate from 35 over to 25 over, dropping from 3 demerit points to 2. Worth the fee alone.
