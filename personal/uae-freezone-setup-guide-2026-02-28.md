## UAE Driver's License — The Process

**Canada is on the RTA exchange list — no theory test, no road test.**

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Requirements | Emirates ID + residency visa + original Alberta license + eye test |
| Eye test | AED 100-150 at RTA-approved optical center (15-30 min) |
| Exchange fee | AED 950-1,500 |
| Total cost | ~AED 1,050-1,650 (~CAD 390-610) |
| Processing | Same day at RTA Customer Happiness Center (1-2 hours) |
| Validity | 10 years |
| Best RTA centers | Al Barsha, Umm Ramool, Deira |

### CRITICAL: License Surrender

**RTA will permanently keep your Alberta license.** You cannot hold both.

**Before traveling to Dubai:**
- Obtain a **certified copy of your Alberta driving record** from Alberta Transportation
- Keep a photocopy/scan of your Alberta license
- This protects you if you ever need to re-obtain a Canadian license later

**Sequence matters:** Get your Alberta license FIRST (Calgary move), THEN exchange it for UAE license in Dubai. This way you exchange an Alberta license (clean record) rather than a Quebec one.

## Tax — The Critical Warning

### While Maintaining Calgary Home: You Are Still a Canadian Tax Resident

- CRA taxes worldwide income including UAE company income
- UAE Free Zone company = Controlled Foreign Affiliate (CFA) under Canadian tax law
- FAPI rules apply — passive income taxed in Canada on accrual basis
- Active business income may ALSO be FAPI because UAE is not a "Designated Treaty Country" — this is a significant trap
- Canada-UAE tax treaty exists BUT tie-breaker provisions only apply to UAE nationals, not Canadian expats
- T1134 filing required annually (foreign affiliate information return) — penalties for non-filing are significant

### What This Means Practically

- **Do NOT set up the UAE entity to "save taxes"** while maintaining Calgary residency — it will not work
- **DO set up the UAE entity** for: Emirates ID, banking, operational presence, Africa deal-making, travel flexibility, long-term optionality
- **Engage a Canadian international tax lawyer BEFORE incorporating** — firms: BDO, KPMG, Thorsteinssons, or a boutique cross-border specialist
- Budget $2,000-5,000 for proper structuring advice — worth every dollar given CRA enforcement

### If You Eventually Become Non-Resident of Canada

- Sever ALL Canadian ties (sell Calgary home, cancel health card, close accounts)
- File CRA Departure Return + pay departure tax on accrued gains
- Then UAE income is truly tax-free
- This is a future option, not the current plan
