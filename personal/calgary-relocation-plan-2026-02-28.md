## Why This Works

### Driving Record: Clean Slate Confirmed

Quebec is **NOT in the Canadian Driver License Compact (CDLC)**. Alberta cannot pull your SAAQ record. When you exchange your Quebec license for an Alberta one:

- Your Alberta record **starts at zero demerit points**
- No SAAQ data is imported
- Your Alberta driving abstract shows only Alberta events from the transfer date forward
- The pending Quebec speeding ticket (if convicted after you move) stays on your old Quebec record — **Alberta is never notified**

### Tax Savings: Substantial

**Combined Federal + Provincial Marginal Tax Rates:**

| Income | Alberta | Quebec | Annual Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| $100K | 30.5% | 36.1% | ~$5,600 |
| $150K | 36.0% | 45.7% | ~$14,500 |
| $200K | 38.0% | 47.5% | ~$19,000 |
| $250K+ | 42-43% | 50-53% | ~$20,000-$50,000+ |

**Plus:** Alberta has zero provincial sales tax (vs Quebec's 9.975% QST on every purchase).

### Alberta Demerit System

- Suspension threshold: 15 points (same as Quebec for age 25+)
- Points expire: 2 years from conviction
- **Bonus:** Alberta lets you remove up to 3 points by completing a Defensive Driving Course (once every 2 years) — Quebec has NO equivalent

## What Happens to the Pending Quebec Ticket

If convicted **after** you hold an Alberta license:
- SAAQ records it on your old Quebec file
- Quebec does **not** notify Alberta (no sharing agreement)
- Your Alberta record is **unaffected**
- Your Alberta insurance is **unaffected** (unless you voluntarily disclosed it)
- Only issue: if you ever return to Quebec and try to register a vehicle, unpaid fines would block you

## Tax Residency — The Key Rule

**Province of residence on December 31 = your tax province for the ENTIRE year.**

CRA does not pro-rate between provinces. If you move to Alberta in October and are there on December 31 — the full year is taxed at Alberta rates. This is the single most important date.

### Splitting Time Between Calgary, UAE, and Africa

- You remain a **Canadian tax resident** as long as you maintain significant ties to Canada (home, business)
- Alberta residency is confirmed by your lease, health card, license, and banking being in Alberta
- Time abroad does NOT sever Canadian residency — but you pay Canadian tax at Alberta rates, not Quebec rates
- If you eventually want to become a **non-resident of Canada entirely** (UAE-based), that's a separate, more complex process — don't conflate with this move
