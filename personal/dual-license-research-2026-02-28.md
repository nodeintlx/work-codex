# Second Driver's License Research — Quebec Resident
**Date:** 2026-02-28 | **For:** Makir Volcy (personal)
**Conclusion:** No legal path to a second license while remaining a Quebec resident.

---

## Bottom Line

Every avenue for a "second clean license" either requires **genuine relocation** (which means surrendering your Quebec license, not adding to it) or constitutes **fraud with serious criminal exposure** (up to 10 years under Criminal Code s. 403). The real tools for protecting your record are narrower but fully legal.

---

## Why a Second License Doesn't Work

| Option | Result |
|---|---|
| Another Canadian province | Illegal — both provinces cancel the other upon application |
| US state | Not possible — requires legal state residency |
| Caribbean (Haiti, DR, etc.) | Requires actual residency; tourist permits are temporary |
| European country | Requires genuine relocation + surrendering Quebec license |
| International Driving Permit | Translation document only — not a license, no separate record |

**Key risk:** If you hold two licenses through misrepresentation, an insurer can **void your policy after an accident**, and you face potential identity fraud charges.

## The Hidden Advantage: Quebec Is NOT in the Canadian Driver License Compact

This is the most useful finding. Quebec only shares driving records with **three jurisdictions**:

| Shares records with Quebec | Does NOT share records with Quebec |
|---|---|
| Ontario | British Columbia |
| New York State | Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba |
| Maine | All other provinces/territories |
| | Florida, Texas, California, all other US states |

**What this means:** A speeding ticket in BC, Alberta, Florida, or most of the world does NOT go on your Quebec SAAQ record. Only Ontario, New York, and Maine convictions transfer.

Exception: Criminal Code offences (impaired driving, dangerous driving) are shared everywhere.

## Legitimate Record Protection Strategies

### 1. Contest Every Ticket (Most Powerful)
- Points only applied upon conviction — contesting suspends this
- Traffic lawyers: $200-500 flat fee, routinely get dismissals or reductions
- Even reducing 35 over to 25 over saves a demerit point

### 2. Avoid Tickets in Ontario, NY, and Maine
- Only three jurisdictions that report to your Quebec record
- Everywhere else: tickets stay local

### 3. Wait the 2-Year Expiry
- Points expire 2 years from conviction date
- No new offences during that period
- Monitor at SAAQclic (saaq.gouv.qc.ca)

### 4. Monitor Your SAAQ Record
- Log into SAAQclic regularly
- Know your current balance and conviction dates
- Dispute errors promptly

### 5. SAAQ Hearing if Facing Suspension
- Request a hearing before revocation takes effect
- Hardship license available in exceptional cases
- Retain a traffic law specialist (avocat en droit routier)

**Note:** Quebec has NO defensive driving course that removes points — unlike some US states or Nova Scotia.

---

## IDP (International Driving Permit) — Worth Getting for Travel

- $32 from CAA-Quebec, same-day issuance
- Valid 1 year, must carry with your Quebec license
- Useful for driving abroad (Africa, Europe, etc.) — relevant for NRG Bloom travel
- Does NOT create a separate record

## Demerit Point Quick Reference

| Points | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Current ticket (35 over) | 3 points |
| Suspension (age 25+) | 15 points |
| Suspension (age 23-24) | 12 points |
| Points expiry | 2 years from conviction |
| Foreign license holder in QC | Only 4 points before suspension (worse!) |
