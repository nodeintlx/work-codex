## Five Legal Theories Available

### 1. Breach of Contract
If the Agreement contained non-circumvention obligations (express or implied). Straightforward theory. Damages = expectation (what NRG Bloom would have earned had TON performed).

### 2. Breach of Duty of Honest Performance
**Key theory.** The Supreme Court of Canada established in **Bhasin v. Hrynew (2014 SCC 71)** — an Alberta-originating case — that ALL commercial contracts carry a duty of honest performance. Parties must not lie or knowingly mislead about matters linked to contract performance.

Expanded by **C.M. Callow Inc. v. Zollinger (2020 SCC 45)** to cover half-truths, omissions, and silence. TON engaging NRG Bloom as if the partnership was intact while secretly building direct Axxela relationships = exactly the *Callow* scenario.

Damages: expectation damages (what NRG Bloom would have earned under honest performance).

### 3. Breach of Fiduciary Duty
If the relationship was partnership-like (joint venture, shared trust over key relationships), fiduciary duties may apply. **This is the most powerful theory** because it unlocks **disgorgement of ALL of TON's profits** — not just NRG Bloom's share.

Key precedents:
- **Strother v. 3464920 Canada Inc. (2007 SCC 24)** — disgorgement for breach of fiduciary duty
- **GasTOPS Ltd. v. Forsyth (2012 ONCA 134)** — $12M+ disgorgement (10-year period)
- **WSIB Investments v. Plenary Group (2024)** — $259M disgorgement
- **Extreme Venture Partners v. Varma** — $29.5M USD disgorgement

Courts are cautious about finding fiduciary duties in arm's-length commercial relationships. The relationship must involve genuine trust and discretion — NRG Bloom entrusting TON with its key relationships (Axxela, Ogboinbiri community) supports this.

### 4. Breach of Confidence
NRG Bloom shared confidential information (contacts, site intelligence, gas supply terms) that TON used to establish direct relationships. The landmark SCC case **International Corona Resources v. LAC Minerals ([1989] 2 SCR 574)** supports a constructive trust remedy — TON could be forced to hand over profits from the operation entirely.

### 5. Tortious Interference with Economic Relations (Unlawful Means Tort)
Established in **AI Enterprises Ltd v. Bram Enterprises Ltd. (2014 SCC 12)**. Elements: (1) intent to injure, (2) interference by unlawful means, (3) economic loss. The pre-meditated circumvention evidence supports intent. However, the "unlawful means" element is narrow — must be independently actionable.

- Direct expenditures: ~N68.4M (~$47,127 USD)
- Stacks on top of lost profit claims

## Jurisdiction — Why Alberta Works

**TON is an Alberta company.** This is a presumptive connecting factor under the SCC's *Club Resorts* test. Alberta courts have jurisdiction over Alberta defendants for claims arising from their business conduct, even if operations are abroad.

**Marley's own statement** inviting NRG Bloom to pursue non-circumvention in Alberta is on the record — undermines any forum non conveniens argument.

**Enforceability:** An Alberta judgment against an Alberta company is directly enforceable against TON's Alberta assets. No need to go to Nigeria to enforce.

## Critical Risks and Considerations

### 1. Limitation Period (URGENT)
Alberta has a **2-year basic limitation period** from when NRG Bloom knew or should have known of the breach. Circumvention appears to have started mid-2025 — well within window. **Must be confirmed with Sarit Batner on March 6 call.** If any risk of clock running out, file Statement of Claim immediately to preserve rights.

### 2. Arbitration Clause
Section 7 of the Agreement contains a dispute resolution mechanism. If it includes mandatory arbitration, TON can apply to stay court action. **Sarit needs to review exact contract language.** However:
- Tort claims (breach of confidence, tortious interference) may fall outside a narrowly-worded arbitration clause
- Fiduciary duty claims may also be outside scope
- *Uber v. Heller* (2020 SCC) allows courts to void unconscionable arbitration clauses

### 3. Quantum Speculation Risk
Lost profits in a startup Nigerian crypto mining operation are inherently speculative. Courts require "reasonable certainty." **Expert evidence essential** — financial expert to produce lost profits analysis anchored to TON's own $264K/month model.

### 4. Discovery Risk
Litigation involves mutual disclosure. If NRG Bloom has internal communications that undercut its narrative, TON's counsel will find them. **Review all internal comms before filing.**
