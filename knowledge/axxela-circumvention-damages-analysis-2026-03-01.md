## The $15M PPA — What It Actually Means

The $15.3M figure is the **total cost of power** over 5 years (6.74 GWh/month @ $0.03/kWh x 60 months). This is the INPUT cost — what it costs to buy electricity from Axxela to run the mining operation. This is NOT what NRG Bloom would have earned.

The relevant figure for NRG Bloom's damages is **David Billay's financial model**: $264,668/month in operational profit with NRG Bloom included in the split ("axxella 25 nrgbloom/ton"). Over 5 years, that's potentially **~$15.9M in total operational profit** to be divided between parties.

The $15M PPA value serves as **context** — it demonstrates the scale and seriousness of the opportunity. The Billay model is what quantifies the actual profit potential.

## Two Separate Heads of Damage

### 1. The Act of Circumvention (The Wrong Itself)

The **breach occurs the moment TON dealt directly with Axxela, bypassing NRG Bloom** who originated the relationship. It doesn't matter whether the Axxela deal was ultimately built out or not.

Under non-circumvention principles, the act itself is the breach. The obligation exists to protect business relationships from exactly this kind of conduct.

**Key evidence:**
- Tyler talking to Franklin "almost daily" — David Billay admission, Jun 11 (13 days before documented circumvention)
- Franklin (Axxela MD) had no idea Makir was being excluded: "I thought the teams are working together" (Jun 28)
- Marley's Jul 4 email exploiting Franklin's false assumption
- Progressive reduction of NRG Bloom's share was the mechanism of the circumvention

### 2. The Destruction of NRG Bloom's Opportunity (The Loss)

This is about what NRG Bloom lost — the ability to develop a real deal with Axxela that NRG Bloom originated.

Falls under **"loss of opportunity" / "lost chance" doctrine**. The legal question:

> *What was the realistic probability that NRG Bloom would have completed this deal, and what would it have been worth?*

**Evidence showing HIGH probability:**
- The PPA was received — real document, real numbers ($0.03/kWh)
- Axxela confirmed 6MW available capacity
- Makir created the "Axxela 10MW Development" group chat
- David Billay built a financial model that included NRG Bloom as partner
- Franklin (Axxela MD) believed the teams were working together
- Nana Umebese (Axxela BD) used "from your end" — showing they saw NRG Bloom as the relationship
- Axxela's own meeting minutes described TON as "investors from Canada" — not originators

This was NOT speculative. This was a deal in active development with documented terms, confirmed pricing, and a financial model built by TON's own team member.

## Does It Matter Whether TON Built Out the Axxela Deal?

### If TON executed the Axxela deal without NRG Bloom:
- Damages are clearer and stronger
- Can point to actual revenue TON is earning from a relationship NRG Bloom originated
- The circumvention produced a concrete, measurable gain for TON and a concrete, measurable loss for NRG Bloom

### If TON did NOT execute the Axxela deal:
- NRG Bloom still has a valid claim — framed as lost opportunity
- The PPA, financial model, confirmed capacity all prove the opportunity was real and valuable
- Courts regularly award damages for lost opportunities when evidence shows the deal was probable, not just possible
- Even if TON hasn't built it yet, they may still be sitting on the direct Axxela relationship as a valuable asset — ready to develop without NRG Bloom
- The circumvention gave TON a valuable asset (direct access to Axxela) that properly derives from NRG Bloom's origination

## Intent Absolutely Matters

Intent affects **how severely** a court or arbitrator treats the conduct. What NRG Bloom has documented is not a misunderstanding — it's a **pattern of deliberate conduct**:

1. Tyler cultivated Franklin behind Makir's back for weeks before documented circumvention
2. They progressively reduced NRG Bloom's share (1MW ownership → management fee → 8% → 0%)
3. Tyler refused the operations fee in December, then they terminated for non-operational status in February — manufactured grounds
4. The Billay financial model proves they understood NRG Bloom's role as a partner, then chose to eliminate NRG Bloom anyway

This pattern supports claims for **aggravated damages** — the idea that the wrongdoer's conduct was so deliberate and calculated that damages should be higher than the bare economic loss.

It's the difference between "we made a business mistake" and "we deliberately stripped your value and threw you out."
