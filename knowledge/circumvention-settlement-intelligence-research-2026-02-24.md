## ANGLE 1: CIRCUMVENTION CASE PRECEDENTS — ENERGY/MINING/INFRASTRUCTURE

### Leading Case: Energy Venture Partners Ltd v. Malabu Oil and Gas Ltd [2013] EWHC 2118 (Comm)

**Facts:** EVP, a BVI company, introduced buyers to Malabu's Nigerian oil block OPL245 and was cut out of the deal. The total transaction was approximately $1.1–1.3 billion (Malabu received $1.1B from the Nigerian government; Shell and Eni paid $1.3B for the block). EVP had no fully executed formal fee agreement — only a written "exclusivity agreement" with no fee mechanism, plus oral promises.

**Outcome:** English Commercial Court awarded EVP **$110.5 million** on a quantum meruit / implied contract basis. This is approximately **8.5–10% of the deal value**. The court found an implied contract for services even without an express fee clause, valued the fee at what was "just and reasonable" for the introduction services rendered.

**What makes this directly analogous to NRG Bloom:** NRG Bloom introduced TON to the Axxela opportunity, built the financial model, negotiated the power arrangement, and secured community/site access. These are services of demonstrable commercial value. Even if TON argues the JVA is ambiguous, NRG Bloom wins on implied contract / quantum meruit — the court will ask what reasonable compensation for those services would be.

**EVP also obtained a Mareva freezing order** that caused Malabu to pay $215 million into court as security. This is a direct playbook: a freezing order on TON's Canadian assets would force a settlement before trial.

**Key data point for your claim:** EVP awarded ~8.5–10% of $1.1–1.3B deal value. Applied to NRG Bloom: If the Axxela 10MW deal is valued at $5.1M CapEx or ~$55M total 10-year revenue, 8.5–10% = **$433K–$5.5M**. If valued at the full 10-year NRG Bloom profit stream of $31.76M, the comparable range is $2.7M–$3.2M. These converge around the **$3M–$5.5M settlement band**.

### Secondary Case: Premia Marketing Ltd v. Regis Mutual Management Ltd [2021] EWHC 2329 (QB)

**Facts:** Premia introduced Regis to a client (The Caravan Club) for a four-year mutual insurance scheme. No written agreement. Regis stated in emails that "Premia would receive some financial reward if the introduction bore fruit." Regis refused to pay.

**Outcome:** Court found implied contract under s.15 Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982. Awarded Premia **£212,000 plus 10% of Regis's net profit per year** from the client. Critically, the court valued the fee not on time spent but on **commercial value generated for the defendant**.

**Direct application to NRG Bloom:** TON's emails and WhatsApp messages acknowledging NRG Bloom's role in securing the Axxela site and power arrangement are the equivalent of the "some financial reward" emails in Premia. The court will assess NRG Bloom's compensation based on the value TON would extract from the Axxela deal — i.e., TON's profit stream, not just NRG Bloom's costs. This supports a claim for a **percentage of TON's ongoing Axxela revenue**, not just a one-time payment.

### Negative Precedent to Avoid: Barton v. Morris [2023] UKSC

**Facts:** Introducer had oral agreement to be paid £1.2M if he introduced a buyer who purchased property for more than £6.5M. Buyer purchased for £6M. Court found no entitlement because the "strike price" condition was not met. No quantum meruit either because the parties had explicitly addressed payment (contingent on strike price).

**What this means for NRG Bloom:** TON will argue NRG Bloom's entitlement is conditional on continued JV participation, not triggered by introduction alone. Counter-argument: The JVA Section 5 exclusivity clause is not conditioned on any strike price — it is a blanket covenant. The circumvention is the breach, not a failure to meet a condition. Distinguish Barton by pointing to the unconditional nature of the Section 5 exclusivity obligation.

## ANGLE 2: PRE-LITIGATION DISCLOSURE MECHANISMS

### (A) Norwich Pharmacal Order (NPO)

**What it does:** Forces a third party (Axxela) who has been "mixed up" in the circumvention to disclose the terms of its agreement with TON, including deal value, payment structure, hashrate allocation, and power pricing.

**Applicable jurisdictions:**

- **UK:** Axxela's new owners (BlueCore consortium) include Stanbic IBTC (Standard Bank Group) — both have UK-regulated entities. An NPO against Standard Bank's UK entity could compel disclosure of the Axxela-TON deal terms.
- **Canada:** Norwich Order is recognized. Since TON is Canadian and the JVA is governed by Canadian law, a Canadian court has strong grounds to order disclosure.
- **Nigeria:** NPO is recognized under Lagos State High Court law as an equitable remedy. A Nigerian NPO against Axxela directly would compel production of the TON agreement, gas supply terms, and power pricing. File with a "gag order" to prevent Axxela alerting TON before the order is executed.

**Four conditions for NPO (Collier v. Bennett [2020]):**
1. Arguable wrong: the circumvention of JVA Section 5 — clearly established
2. Mixed up in: Axxela negotiated directly with TON knowing the NRG Bloom relationship existed
3. Possession: Axxela holds the TON agreement and all deal terms
4. Overall justice: Proportionate — need the deal value to quantify damages

**Practical note:** An NPO against Axxela in Nigeria is likely the fastest path. Lagos courts can issue ex parte. File alongside a gag order.

### (B) Anton Piller Order — Nigeria

Authorizes a search of TON's Nigerian premises or Axxela's offices for documents relating to the TON-Axxela arrangement, without prior notice. Nuclear option — use if NPO is resisted or if evidence destruction is suspected.

**Nigerian precedent:** First granted in Ferodo Limited v. Unibros Stores (1980). Routinely granted in Lagos courts.

### (C) Canadian Interlocutory Injunction — The Primary Tool

**Test:** RJR-MacDonald Inc. v. Canada (AG) [1994] — three parts:
1. **Serious issue to be tried:** Section 5 of the JVA is unambiguous. Easily satisfied.
2. **Irreparable harm:** If TON executes an Axxela deal without NRG Bloom and begins generating mining revenue, NRG Bloom's loss ($264,668/month) is ongoing and not easily quantified or reversed.
3. **Balance of convenience:** NRG Bloom suffers ongoing monthly losses; TON loses only delay.

**Settlement effect:** An injunction makes the Axxela deal unbankable and unexecutable for TON. Axxela's new institutional owners (Afreximbank, Standard Bank, Stanbic IBTC) cannot proceed with a supplier under injunction. TON will settle within 30–60 days.

### (D) Nigerian Emergency Arbitrator (AMA 2023)

Under the new Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023, an emergency arbitrator can be appointed within **2 business days** to issue interim relief before the main tribunal is constituted (Section 16).
