## KEY COMPETITORS

### Harvey AI — $11B valuation, $195M ARR
- Pricing: $100-$500/user/month; $30K-$300K+/year minimums
- Capabilities: Legal research, memo drafting, contract review
- Does NOT: multi-channel evidence synthesis, deploy vs. hold strategy, WhatsApp analysis

### CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters) — 1M users
- Pricing: $225-$428/user/month (requires Westlaw)
- Capabilities: Case law research, document summarization, deposition prep
- Does NOT: multi-source cross-referencing, strategy layer

### Relativity aiR — Enterprise eDiscovery
- Pricing: $50K-$200K+ implementation; aiR now bundled
- Capabilities: Document review, chronology, key fact extraction, witness prep
- Closest functional competitor but enterprise-only
- Does NOT: WhatsApp/chat analysis, contradiction detection, strategy advice

### EvenUp — $2B valuation
- Personal injury demand packages only. Not relevant for commercial disputes.

### AAA Resolution Simulator — Launched March 4, 2026
- Built by AAA + McKinsey QuantumBlack
- Simulates arbitrator decisions from documents
- Does NOT: multi-channel synthesis, contradiction detection, evidence strategy

### Luminance, Spellbook, Kira Systems
- Contract-focused. Not litigation tools.

## MARKET GAPS COLDSTORM FILLS

1. Multi-channel evidence synthesis (WhatsApp + email + calls + contracts) — nobody does this
2. Deploy vs. hold strategy — nobody does this
3. Cross-party contradiction detection — nobody does this
4. Per-engagement pricing for mid-market — Harvey/Relativity are enterprise-only
5. Emerging market commercial arbitration — zero competitors in Africa
6. Privilege-preserving architecture — SDNY Feb 2026 ruling makes this critical

## KEY REGULATORY DEVELOPMENT

SDNY Judge Rakoff ruled Feb 2026: prompts/outputs from public AI tools are NOT attorney-client privileged. This creates a sales trigger for on-premise/hybrid deployment.
