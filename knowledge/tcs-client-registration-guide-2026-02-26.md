## Bottom Line Up Front

The TCS does not have a bureaucratic "client application form." There is no online form to submit and wait for approval. The process is relationship-driven: you contact the Montreal regional office (your home base), have an introductory meeting with a sector-appropriate trade commissioner, get assessed informally, and if you qualify, you become an active client immediately. The whole process can happen in under a week.

MyTCS (the online portal) is a mailing list / information service — not the formal client onboarding mechanism. You register for MyTCS to receive tailored updates. You become a TCS client by contacting your regional office directly.

## The Two-Track Registration Process

### Track A — MyTCS Portal (do this first, takes 5 minutes)
- **URL:** https://www.tradecommissioner.gc.ca/en/mytcs-registration/canadian-company.html
- Select "Canadian company" as your category
- Select sectors: Clean Technology, Energy/Cleantech, ICT/Digital Infrastructure
- Select target markets: Nigeria / Sub-Saharan Africa
- No documents required
- This gives you: personalized content, events, webinars, guides
- This does NOT give you: an assigned trade commissioner or active client services

### Track B — Formal Client Relationship (the real thing)
- Contact the Montreal regional office directly
- Or use the "Find a Trade Commissioner" tool: https://www.tradecommissioner.gc.ca/trade-commissioner-delegue-commercial/search-recherche.aspx?lang=eng
- Ask for a TC who covers Clean Technology or ICT/Digital Infrastructure with a Sub-Saharan Africa focus
- Have an introductory meeting — they assess export readiness informally
- Once assessed, you are an active client with a file in their system

## Eligibility Requirements

**For general TCS client services (no hard revenue/employee minimums):**
- Must be a Canadian company or organization
- Must primarily deal in Canadian products or services
- Must be "export-ready" — management commitment, financial resources, market research done
- Must have potential to contribute to Canada's economy through increased exports
- No published minimum revenue or employee count for basic TCS client status

**For CanExport SMEs funding (separate, harder bar):**
- Annual revenue between $100,000 and $100 million CAD
- For-profit corporation
- Active CRA Business Number
- Planning to enter a new market with little/no existing sales

**NRG Bloom assessment:** Qualifies for TCS client services based on Canadian incorporation, active Nigeria target market, and the Oando partnership as a concrete lead. CanExport SMEs revenue threshold ($100K) may be an issue — confirm with TC.

## Why Montreal First, Then Lagos

The protocol is: Montreal regional office first, then they introduce you to TCS Lagos. This warm handoff is far more effective than cold-emailing Lagos directly.

- Montreal creates your client file and assesses your needs
- Montreal formally introduces you to TCS Lagos
- Lagos prioritizes clients with established TCS relationships
- You can still send the Lagos email in parallel, but reference your Montreal registration

- Your international business development plan
- What advantages your product/service has over competitors
- Export readiness: management commitment, financial resources, market research
- Whether you have existing sales leads or opportunities abroad (Oando = yes)
