## Bottom Line Up Front

- **Filing NIL T2 returns yourself is legal and achievable at near-zero cost.** CloudTax offers a genuine $0 pay-what-you-want option for NIL T2 returns.
- **CO-17 (Quebec) has no free electronic option,** but paper filing is permitted for NRG Bloom (revenue under $1M) and is free except postage.
- **Your biggest financial risk is Quebec's $25/day failure-to-file penalty** (max $2,500 per year per unfiled CO-17) — not the T2 late-filing penalty, which is $0 when no tax is owing.
- **NRG Bloom is federally incorporated** — this means you have four separate filing obligations: CRA T2, Revenu Quebec CO-17, Corporations Canada annual return, and REQ annual declaration.
- **Corporations Canada annual returns are urgent.** At $12/each, overdue returns (4 years = $48) must be filed before dissolution risk becomes real.
- **Act immediately on the status checks** — you may already be at or near dissolution threshold at both Corporations Canada and REQ.

## Part 1 — Your Four Filing Obligations

As a federal CBCA corporation operating in Quebec, NRG Bloom has four distinct annual filing obligations:

### 1. CRA — T2 Corporation Income Tax Return
- **Due:** 6 months after fiscal year-end (e.g., June 30 if Dec 31 year-end)
- **Filing method:** Electronic (mandatory for tax years starting after Jan 1, 2024; older years can be paper but e-file recommended)
- **Cost to file:** $0 with CloudTax NIL return option
- **Late penalty when $0 tax owing:** $0 (formula-based: 5% of unpaid tax; if $0 owing, $0 penalty)
- **Risk:** CRA demand to file, arbitrary assessment if never filed

### 2. Revenu Quebec — CO-17 Corporation Income Tax Return
- **Due:** 6 months after fiscal year-end (same deadline as T2)
- **Filing method:** Paper permitted if revenue < $1M (which NRG Bloom qualifies for 2021-2022)
- **Cost to file:** $0 if filing on paper
- **Late penalty when $0 tax owing:** $0 on percentage-based calculation, BUT separate $25/day failure-to-file penalty (max $2,500/year) applies regardless of balance
- **Risk:** Up to $2,500 per year per unfiled return

### 3. Corporations Canada — Annual Return (CBCA)
- **Due:** Within 60 days of corporation anniversary date each year
- **Filing method:** Online only at Corporations Canada portal
- **Cost:** $12 per year
- **Late penalty:** No direct financial penalty, but "overdue" status means no Certificate of Compliance; dissolution risk after ~3 years
- **Risk:** Administrative dissolution

### 4. REQ — Annual Updating Declaration
- **Due:** Varies (typically within the filing period each year)
- **Filing method:** Online or via joint filing with CO-17 (check box on CO-17 line 39)
- **Cost:** Approximately $104/year for a Quebec business corporation
- **Late penalty:** 5% of unpaid fee + 1%/month (max 12 months)
- **Risk:** Dissolution after 2 consecutive missed declarations

## Part 3 — T2 Federal Return (NIL): Complete Process

### Eligibility for T2 Short Return

NRG Bloom likely qualifies for the simpler T2 Short Return for 2021 and 2022. Requirements (all must be met):
- CCPC throughout the year: Yes (federally incorporated, Canadian-controlled)
- Nil net income or a loss: Yes (NIL year)
- Permanent establishment in only one province: Yes (Quebec)
- Not claiming refundable tax credits: Yes (NIL year, no credits)
- No taxable dividends paid or received: Yes (NIL year)
- Reports in Canadian dollars: Yes

### Required Forms and Schedules

For NIL T2 Short:
- T2 Short Return (2 pages — the main document)
- Schedule 1 — Net Income/Loss Reconciliation ($0 for NIL)
- Schedule 100 — Balance Sheet (GIFI codes; all $0 or nominal)
- Schedule 125 — Income Statement (GIFI; all $0)
- Schedule 141 — Notes Checklist
- Schedule 50 — Shareholder Information (your name, % ownership)
- Schedule 101 — Opening Balance Sheet (first year of filing only = 2021)

### Information Required

- Corporation name and Business Number (BN) — 9 digits from CRA
- Fiscal year start and end dates
- Province of permanent establishment: Quebec
- Type of corporation: CCPC
- SIN of signing officer (you)
- Simple financial statements for each year (balance sheet + income statement; all $0 for NIL)
- NAICS code (518210 for data centers or appropriate code)

### Electronic Filing — How to Get Your Web Access Code (WAC)

Option 1: Through CRA My Business Account (once registered)
- Log in → select your corporation → find "Web Access Code"
- WAC is entered into your T2 software when transmitting

Option 2: Call CRA Business Inquiries: 1-800-959-5525

### Late Filing for 2021 and 2022

- You can file these returns in 2026 — CRA accepts late returns with no time limit for NIL returns
- For 2021 tax year: use software that supports 2021 (FutureTax 2024.2 covers from Jan 1, 2022; you may need an older version for 2021)
- For 2022 tax year: FutureTax 2024.2 covers this (range: Jan 1, 2022 to May 31, 2025)
- CloudTax: confirm which back years are supported

**Important note on 2021:** If NRG Bloom's fiscal year is January 1, 2021 to December 31, 2021, you need software that covers a tax year starting in 2021. FutureTax 2024.2 starts from Jan 1, 2022 — so you may need their older version. Check https://www.futuretax.ca/t2/download.php

## Part 4 — CO-17 Quebec Return (NIL): Complete Process

### Is CO-17 Required for NIL Years?
Yes. Absolutely mandatory. No exceptions.

### Paper Filing Option (Free)

Since NRG Bloom had revenue under $1M in 2021-2022, you may file CO-17 on paper.

1. Download CO-17 form (French and English versions):
   URL: https://www.revenuquebec.ca/en/online-services/forms-and-publications/current-details/co-17-t/
2. Complete: identification, fiscal year, corporation type, all income/expense lines ($0), balance sheet ($0)
3. On line 39: check the joint filing box if your REQ information is current (combines with annual updating declaration)
4. Attach: Simple balance sheet and income statement (can be a one-page internal document; does not need to be audited)
5. Note: Quebec requires financial statements and some forms to be in French. For a NIL return, a simple bilingual or French-only statement is sufficient.
6. Mail to Revenu Quebec

Mail address options:
- Montreal: Revenu Québec, Case postale 3000, succursale Place-Desjardins, Montréal (Québec) H5B 1A4
- Quebec City: Direction principale des services aux entreprises, 3800, rue de Marly, Québec, QC G1X 4A5

### Electronic Filing Option (~$150 with UFile T2)

If you prefer to file CO-17 electronically:
1. Purchase UFile T2: https://www.ufile.ca/products/ufilet2
2. Register for Revenu Quebec My Account for Businesses and get NetFile Quebec access
3. Complete both T2 and CO-17 in the same software
4. Transmit both electronically

### Quebec Late Filing — The $25/Day Penalty Reality

Even if no tax is owing, Quebec's Tax Administration Act imposes:
- $25/day from when the return was due
- Minimum: $100
- Maximum: $2,500 per return per year

**Practical advice:** File all back CO-17 returns immediately. The penalty clock has been running since the due dates (typically June 30 of each year). Filing now cannot undo past accrual, but it stops the clock and demonstrates good faith. Revenu Quebec has a Voluntary Disclosure / Relief program — enquire about penalty reduction when filing.

Source: https://www.revenuquebec.ca/en/one-mission-concrete-actions/ensuring-tax-compliance/penalties-and-interest/penalties/penalty-for-failure-to-file/

## Part 8 — 2023 Filing Considerations

### What's Different vs. NIL Years

2023 had some financial activity — meaning:
- Some expenses were incurred (professional fees, travel, etc.)
- Possibly minimal revenue
- T2 will NOT be NIL — it will be a zero or loss return

### Additional Schedules Needed

On top of the NIL year schedules, you may need:
- **Schedule 4** — Loss continuity (if carrying forward a loss from 2023 to future years)
- **Schedule 8** — Capital Cost Allowance (if you purchased any assets, even small ones like laptops)
- More detailed GIFI entries for actual expenses on Schedule 125
- If any consulting income: Schedule 125 with revenue entries

### Can You Self-File 2023?

**Yes, if:**
- Activity was limited to startup expenses (professional fees, government fees, bank fees)
- No employees on payroll
- No GST/QST collected from customers
- No complex transactions

**Consider hiring someone if:**
- Revenue exceeds $25,000
- You have employees or contractors
- You have foreign income or foreign assets
- R&D expenses overlap with the 2024 SR&ED claim (important — discuss with SR&ED consultant first)
- Multiple bank accounts or credit cards with complex transactions

**Software recommendation for 2023:** UFile T2 (~$150) — handles both T2 and CO-17 electronically, user-friendly interface, supports Quebec filers.

### Records Needed for 2023

- All bank statements (all months)
- All credit card statements for corporate cards
- All receipts for deductible expenses
- Any contracts (for professional services received)
- Any invoices issued (if any revenue)
- Categorize expenses: professional fees, bank charges, travel, office, government fees, etc.
