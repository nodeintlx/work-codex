## CONTRADICTION 1: Government/Private Sector Overlap

**The Issue:** Deputy General Director of Samruk-Kazyna Invest (state SOE subsidiary) simultaneously serving as President of Kun Renewables (private Lancaster Group company) in approximately 2010-2011.

**Research Findings:**

- The February 10, 2011 Inform.kz/Bloomberg article (conducted in Mumbai) identifies Bhamre as "president of the Almaty-based company" Kun Renewables LLP — with no mention of his Samruk-Kazyna title.
- The June 2013 Astana Times interview identifies him as "Deputy General Director of Samruk-Kazyna Invest."
- Kazakhstan law at the time permitted quasi-government employees to work only in entities where the state owned more than 50% of voting shares. Kun Renewables was a Lancaster Group private equity subsidiary — not a state-owned entity.
- The Development Bank of Kazakhstan (a state institution) was lending $200M to the Kun Renewables project. Bhamre was simultaneously an official at Samruk-Kazyna Invest, which was involved in investment facilitation. This creates a direct conflict: a state fund official running a private company receiving state financing.
- Samruk-Kazyna has a Code of Conduct requiring employees to be "free from conflicts of interest," with the broader institutional problem being the state's dual role as shareholder and regulator.
- Kazakhstan only formally lifted business restrictions for civil servants and quasi-government employees in **December 2025**. In 2010-2011, those restrictions were fully in force.

**Assessment:** The concurrent roles are real. Whether the Kun Renewables presidency was disclosed to, or approved by, Samruk-Kazyna is the critical question. The absence of any mention of his Samruk-Kazyna role in the Kun Renewables interviews suggests he may have compartmentalized the two identities.

**Rating: CRITICAL — Warrants direct explanation. Not resolved by available open-source research.**

## CONTRADICTION 5: Education Timeline — Mathematically Impossible as Stated

**The Issue:** M.Eng at University of Toronto ~1987-1990 is inconsistent with DOB August 1968. He would be 18-19 in 1987 — before completing an undergraduate degree.

**Research Findings:**

- An MEng is a postgraduate degree requiring a prior bachelor's. A standard bachelor's degree requires 3-4 years. With a DOB of August 1968, the earliest possible bachelor's completion would be ~1989-1990 (starting university at 18 in 1986, finishing a 3-year degree).
- The University of Toronto MEng can be completed in one year (intensive full-time) or two years (regular pace) currently — similar structure applied historically.
- The Astana Times interview confirms he was "born and brought up for the most part in India" then went to Canada for "further education" — implying an Indian undergraduate degree followed by Canadian graduate study. Indian bachelor's programs are typically 3-4 years.
- A more plausible timeline: bachelor's in India ~1986-1990, M.Eng at U of T ~1990-1992 (age 22-24). Then oil sector in Canada/US ~1992-mid-1990s.
- The report's "~1987-1990" date appears to be an estimate error of 2-3 years. Shifting to ~1990-1992 makes the entire timeline coherent.

**Assessment:** The reported M.Eng dates (~1987-1990) are impossible given DOB August 1968 and the prerequisite nature of graduate programs. The most likely correct dates are ~1990-1992, implying all subsequent dates in the career timeline may be shifted by approximately 2 years. This would make his Harvard MA more like late 1990s to early 2000s, which remains consistent with his ~2004-2005 arrival in Kazakhstan.

**Rating: SIGNIFICANT — Dates need verification. Educational credential check with U of T registrar is warranted. A misrepresented education timeline on official documents would be a compliance red flag.**

## CONTRADICTION 6: $388M Polysilicon Plant — $200M State Loan, Zero Verified Production

**The Issue:** The Development Bank of Kazakhstan committed $200M to Kun Renewables/Lancaster Group's polysilicon plant in Astana. Production was to begin by approximately October 2012. No evidence of construction, production, or public wind-down exists.

**Research Findings:**

- The February 2011 Inform.kz/Bloomberg article is the only public record of this project. No follow-up reporting on construction, groundbreaking, production, or cancellation was found.
- Kazakhstan's solar manufacturing sector as of 2017-2021 consists entirely of Kazatomprom subsidiaries (Astana Solar, Kazakhstan Solar Silicon, KazSilicon) created with French government/CEA consortium support — a completely different lineage from Lancaster Group. These were being sold for $14.2M total in 2017, a fraction of the $388M Kun Renewables projection.
- Lancaster Group's current company profile lists its sectors as oil services, extraction, pharmaceuticals, education, recruitment, and logistics — no mention of solar or polysilicon.
- A critical contextual factor: global polysilicon prices collapsed approximately 80% between 2011 and 2012 due to Chinese manufacturing overcapacity. A project whose economics depended on non-Chinese production costs would have become unviable within months of the February 2011 announcement. This provides a plausible commercial explanation for project cancellation.
- However: the $200M Development Bank of Kazakhstan loan — whether drawn, partially drawn, or cancelled — has no public record. DBK annual reports for 2011-2015 would be the definitive source.

**Assessment:** The polysilicon price collapse of 2011-2012 is the most likely explanation for project abandonment. Global polysilicon spot prices fell from ~$60/kg in 2011 to ~$15/kg by 2012, making any new non-Chinese facility economically unviable. But the absence of any public accounting — no press release cancelling the project, no DBK loan writedown announcement, no Lancaster Group statement — is notable. If the $200M loan was drawn before cancellation, the question of what happened to those funds is open.

**Rating: CRITICAL — The fate of $200M in state financing attached to a project that produced nothing requires definitive resolution. DBK annual reports and Kazakhstani financial press archives are the recommended sources.**

## CONTRADICTION 7: PAMR — Financial Intermediation SIC, Permanent Dormancy, Virtual Office

**The Issue:** PAMR was incorporated for "financial intermediation" but filed as dormant every year for 8 years before compulsory dissolution.

**Research Findings:**

- PAMR LIMITED (No. 08788423): Incorporated November 25, 2013; SIC 64999 (financial intermediation not elsewhere classified); registered address: Business Resource Network, Kenilworth CV8 2GY (confirmed virtual office/mail-forwarding address); dissolved January 11, 2022 by compulsory strike-off (failed to file, not voluntarily wound down).
- SIC 64999 is a known AML red-flag category — banks specifically monitor financial intermediation companies for money laundering risk.
- Filing as dormant while classified as financial intermediary is a structural pattern used to hold a corporate vehicle for transactions not reflected in Companies House filings. A "dormant" company per UK definition means no "accounting transactions" — but introductions, referral agreements, and non-monetary contracts are not accounting transactions and would not appear in dormant accounts.
- Director listed with US address (Medford MA) and UK-registered nominee secretary (WESTA) — a standard offshore-adjacent structure for a non-UK resident maintaining a UK entity.
- Compulsory strike-off (not voluntary dissolution) indicates abandonment — the company was simply left to lapse.

**Assessment:** PAMR has the hallmarks of a financial intermediation shell held in reserve: SIC 64999, virtual office, offshore-adjacent nominee secretary, non-UK resident director, 8 years of dormancy, compulsory lapse rather than affirmative closure. Whether any unrecorded introductions, deal fees, or referral arrangements passed through PAMR in its 8-year existence is unknowable from Companies House records alone. The question of what financial intermediation activity (if any) occurred outside of the "accounting transactions" definition is the key investigative gap.

**Rating: SIGNIFICANT — Shell structure purpose is unexplained. Companies House record alone is insufficient to determine whether PAMR was truly inactive or was used for undisclosed financial intermediation not captured in dormant filings.**
