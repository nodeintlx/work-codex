## A. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NRG Bloom Inc. ("NRG Bloom") seeks settlement of its claims against TON Infrastructure Ltd. ("TON") arising from:

- The Mutual Non-Disclosure and Non-Circumvention Agreement dated January 10, 2025 ("NDA");
- The Joint Venture Agreement dated February 19, 2025 ("JV Agreement");
- The Ogboinbiri 5MW Site Development and Revenue Sharing Agreement dated May 16, 2025 ("SDA"); and
- Addendum No. 1 to the SDA dated July 3, 2025 ("Addendum").

NRG Bloom's case is that TON systematically devalued NRG Bloom's contractual position through a deliberate campaign described by TON's own representative as a **"calculated tactic."** This devaluation followed a clear trajectory:

| Agreement | Date | Revenue Share | Management Fee |
|---|---|---|---|
| JV Agreement | February 19, 2025 | 1MW allocation (~25% equivalent, expressed as 250 ASICs) | USD $9,000/month |
| Interim Agreement | April 2, 2025 | 40% of remaining revenue after 25% community split (~30% effective) | USD $5,000–$7,400/month |
| SDA | May 16, 2025 | 8% of net Bitcoin mining revenue | USD $875/month per container (max $3,500/month) |
| Termination | February 12, 2026 | 0% | $0 (never paid) |

Each reduction coincided with a termination threat or ultimatum. TON manufactured the conditions for termination, misrepresented NRG Bloom's awareness to third parties, circumvented NRG Bloom's introducer role in violation of the NDA and the JV Agreement's exclusivity clause, and terminated the relationship at the precise moment when revenue was about to flow to NRG Bloom.

**NRG Bloom's settlement position is ₦1.2 billion (~US$727,000).** This represents a material discount from NRG Bloom's full arbitration claim (₦1.8 billion to ₦4 billion) and is offered in the interest of prompt resolution.

## C. NRG BLOOM'S RESPONSE TO TON'S ARGUMENTS

TON has raised a number of procedural and technical arguments across its February 20 Response Letter and February 23 Reply. NRG Bloom addresses each below. These arguments do not diminish the factual record or NRG Bloom's entitlement to compensation.

### C.1 Corporate Identity (NRG Bloom Inc. vs. NRG Bloom Services Limited)

**TON argues:** NRG Bloom Inc. (Canada) and NRG Bloom Services Limited (Nigeria, incorporated October 17, 2025) are separate legal entities, creating confusion about which entity is the claimant.

**NRG Bloom's position:** The contracting party to the SDA is NRG Bloom Inc. — a Canadian federal corporation. NRG Bloom Services Limited was incorporated as NRG Bloom Inc.'s Nigerian operating subsidiary to formalise existing operations on the ground. This is standard corporate practice for Canadian entities operating in Nigeria.

TON's own response concedes the point. At Section 2.1.4, TON acknowledges that *"any services rendered by Nigerian-incorporated personnel were done pursuant to NRG Bloom Inc.'s obligations under the Agreement"* — effectively admitting the agency relationship while simultaneously challenging it.

If TON argues the Nigerian entity is legally separate, it weakens its own ability to assert counterclaims against NRG Bloom Inc. — it cannot have it both ways.

**The claimant is NRG Bloom Inc. NRG Bloom Services Limited operated as its authorised agent in Nigeria. The corporate structure does not affect the substance of any claim.**

### C.2 MNDA Jurisdiction (Alberta vs. Nigeria)

**TON argues:** The NDA is governed by Alberta law, so circumvention claims must be adjudicated in Alberta, not Nigeria.

**NRG Bloom's position:** The circumvention is not only an NDA breach — it is also a breach of the **JV Agreement's exclusivity clause** (Section 5: *"TON agrees that all current and future Bitcoin mining projects in Nigeria will be done exclusively with NRG BLOOM"*) and a breach of the implied duty of good faith under the SDA and Nigerian law.

The Axxela circumvention arises directly "out of or in connection with" the SDA and the parties' commercial relationship in Nigeria, bringing it within Section 7's dispute resolution mechanism.

TON's jurisdiction-splitting strategy increases its own exposure. It faces potential proceedings in **both** Alberta and Nigeria — two sets of legal costs and two forums of liability.

### C.3 Section 9 — Termination Right

**TON argues:** Section 9 of the SDA grants an unconditional right to terminate on notice.

**NRG Bloom's position:**

(a) **TON did not comply with its own notice requirements.** The original JV Agreement required 90 days' written notice plus a 30-day cure period. Even the SDA requires notice. TON gave zero days' notice — Jeff Jespersen's February 9 email (*"We'll get back to you on next steps"*) demonstrates no warning was given. The termination followed three days later.

(b) **An unconditional termination right does not extinguish liability for pre-termination conduct.** TON can have the right to terminate and still owe damages for:
- Unjust enrichment (retaining value NRG Bloom created)
- Circumvention (Axxela relationship and JV Agreement exclusivity breach)
- Economic duress (the progressive devaluation pattern)
- Quantum meruit (reasonable value of services already rendered)

(c) **The timing is the evidence.** TON terminated at the precise moment when NRG Bloom's revenue share was about to begin — containers deployed, transformer installed, NRG Bloom had committed to $36,000–$59,000 in electrical remediation, a new custom container, and Canurta had been introduced as institutional equity partner. The progression from 90-day notice (JV) to 30-day (SDA) to zero-day (actual) is itself evidence of the devaluation pattern.

### C.4 Section 5 — Discontinuation Right

**TON argues:** Section 5 of the SDA grants TON an absolute right to discontinue participation after the completion of any phase, with no damages beyond existing revenue sharing.

**NRG Bloom's position:**

(a) **TON terminated during a phase, not after completing one.** The 40ft InfraFlow container was never operational. The transformer was installed but not connected. The electrical remediation was in progress. No phase was "completed."

(b) **Section 5 did not exist in the original JV Agreement.** It appeared only in the May 16 SDA, negotiated under duress after Tyler's "calculated tactic" termination threat — while Makir was in Edmonton on a non-refundable $2,600 flight, staying at Marley's home, having turned down his only other revenue source (Marshall).

(c) **Even if enforceable, Section 5 does not bar claims that exist independently of the SDA** — including quantum meruit, unjust enrichment, and circumvention. These arise from value conferred and rights violated, not from the SDA's compensation structure.

(d) **Unconscionability.** If Section 5 permits TON to consume thirteen months of NRG Bloom's labour, relationships, operational management, and capital expenditure, then terminate paying nothing, the clause is unconscionable and unenforceable under Nigerian law.

### C.5 Affirmation Defence

**TON argues:** NRG Bloom performed for thirteen months without formally objecting to duress, thereby affirming the contract and waiving duress claims.

**NRG Bloom's position:**

(a) **NRG Bloom had no practical alternative.** Having invested substantial time, money, and relationships — and having ended its partnership with Marshall Long (its sole revenue source) to prioritise TON — NRG Bloom was commercially trapped. Continued performance under duress is not free consent.

(b) **The duress was ongoing.** Each new termination threat renewed the coercion. Threats continued through April, May, July, September, and December 2025.

(c) **NRG Bloom did object.** Makir pushed back on the Obrikom circumvention (April 21, 2025), the Axxela exclusion (July 11, 2025 — lawyer-approved), the management fee refusal (December 4, 2025), and the communication breakdown (December 24, 2025). NRG Bloom engaged legal counsel as early as July 2025 — seven months before termination.

### C.6 Safety Pretext

**TON argues:** NRG Bloom's 20ft container had genuine electrical deficiencies justifying safety concerns.

**NRG Bloom's position:**

(a) **The 10-month gap.** Tyler Macpherson (an electrical journeyman by trade) and Jeff Jespersen personally inspected NRG Bloom's container in February 2025. No safety concerns were raised. The JV Agreement, the SDA, and the Addendum were all executed for that container without any safety objection. Safety concerns appeared only in December 2025 — ten months later.

(b) **The December 3 miners arrived, December 22 first explanation.** When miners arrived at the site on December 3, TON refused to place them in NRG Bloom's container. The first indication was a message from Marley that the electrical infrastructure "isn't good enough," but NRG Bloom received no substantive explanation until Tyler's call on December 22, 2025 — nearly three weeks later.

(c) **TON manufactured impossible deadlines.** Jeff's December 23 meeting minutes imposed action items due December 24–30 — requiring third-party electrical review during the Christmas period in rural Nigeria. These were marked "OVERDUE" on December 31. Makir rightly disputed these as "dates set unilaterally."

(d) **TON refused the fee that would have funded remediation.** On December 4, Marley refused the operations fee: *"not sure this site needs to get online."* The very fee that would have funded the electrical corrections.

(e) **NRG Bloom accepted responsibility and was actively remediating.** On January 8, 2026, Makir committed: *"NRG Bloom is prepared to undertake the financial responsibility for the electrical remediation of our container."* TON terminated before giving NRG Bloom a reasonable opportunity to complete the remediation.

### C.7 TON's Counterclaims

TON reserves counterclaims for site maintenance failures, power supply interruptions, safety non-compliance, misappropriation of community fees, and the January 2026 security breach.

**NRG Bloom's position:** These counterclaims are unquantified and contradicted by TON's own communications:

- **Site maintenance:** NRG Bloom maintained the site for thirteen months while TON refused to pay the agreed operations fee.
- **Power supply:** The December 30 shutdown was ordered by Agnes because of TON's own communication failures — documented in Agnes's December 23 formal complaint: *"I am not comfortable with a lack of response or failure to pass information."* Marley's last message in the Marley–Agnes–Makir coordination group was November 19 — over a month of CEO silence during the crisis.
- **Community fee misappropriation:** 25% of revenue was allocated to the community (~$900/month to Nelson's wallet). This was transparent and documented.
- **January 2026 security breach:** This was Ola Olaniyan's sabotage — his agents physically locked the container and transformer. Makir had been warning TON about Ola since August 2025 (*"Everyone is concerned that Ola is holding that container as leverage"* — Marley's own words, August 25, 2025) and was actively pursuing legal remedies. NRG Bloom immediately reported the incident and supported TON's equipment removal decision.

### C.8 Asset Valuation and Confidentiality Threats

In its February 23 Reply, TON suggested that NRG Bloom's inclusion of an asset valuation table may give rise to claims for breach of confidentiality.

**NRG Bloom's position:** The valuation was included solely to contextualise the proportionality of settlement positions — a standard and legitimate feature of without-prejudice settlement correspondence. The introduction of collateral litigation threats into a process that TON itself describes as good faith engagement is inconsistent and unhelpful. NRG Bloom does not intend to be distracted by it.
