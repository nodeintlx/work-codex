## 2. NEW SECTION: B.4 — THE AXXELA CIRCUMVENTION

This warrants separate treatment because of its scale and the strength of the documentary evidence.

**The Axxela opportunity is valued at a minimum of US$15.3 million over five years** based on financial projections jointly developed by both parties. NRG Bloom originated, developed, and negotiated this opportunity over five months before TON systematically excluded NRG Bloom and engaged Axxela directly.

**NRG Bloom was Axxela's sole counterparty for three months (February–April 2025):**

- **February 4, 2025:** Axxela cold-emailed NRG Bloom directly (info@nrgbloom.com): "I am reaching out on behalf of Axxela Limited... to explore a strategic partnership with NRG Bloom in the area of Bitcoin mining using stranded natural gas." TON had zero involvement in originating this relationship.
- **February 7, 2025:** Makir engaged directly with Axxela (active email correspondence with Ndulue on this date).
- **February 25, 2025:** NRG Bloom presented to Axxela's full leadership team, including Franklin Umole (Managing Director) and four other executives. **Axxela's own meeting minutes** describe TON as "investors from Canada" who were "invited to see" NRG Bloom's Bayelsa facility. All next steps were assigned to NRG Bloom and Axxela — not TON.
- **February–April 2025:** NRG Bloom continued as Axxela's primary contact. Makir conducted follow-up meetings, pricing discussions, and site assessments. TON did not have independent engagement with Axxela during this period.
- **April 22, 2025:** Makir proposed the $0.03/kWh rate to Axxela — the exact rate that became the confirmed deal price.
- **May 2025:** Through direct negotiations between Makir and Nana Umebese (Axxela's Head of Business Development), NRG Bloom secured the $0.03/kWh rate — subsequently confirmed by Axxela on June 12, 2025. TON later attempted to renegotiate this rate down to $0.028/kWh but failed and ultimately accepted the rate Makir had secured.

**Joint engagement (May–June 12, 2025):**

- **May 25, 2025:** Makir organized the first joint Axxela–TON meeting, sending the calendar invite directly to Franklin Umole and Nana Umebese.
- **May 26, 2025:** Axxela sent the gas composition document to both Tyler and Makir, addressing them as "Dear Tyler/Makir" — equal counterparties.
- **June 12, 2025:** Axxela confirmed the deal terms to both Tyler and Makir: $0.03/kWh, 12.5% share of topline bitcoin revenue, 6MW available for immediate deployment. Tyler replied expressing excitement, CC'ing Makir.

**The financial model jointly developed by both parties valued NRG Bloom as a revenue partner, not a finder.** The "Model Explained" document and working spreadsheets show revenue allocations that include NRG Bloom as a core participant in the Axxela deal economics — not as a party entitled to a flat fee.

**The circumvention (June 18 – July 4, 2025):**

- **June 17:** Last email including Makir — David Billay → all parties including Makir; RE: Response to Commercial Proposal.
- **June 18:** David Billay (TON) replies in the same thread but removes Makir from all recipients. This is the first exclusion.
- **June 18 – July 2:** Over the next 15 days, 15 emails are exchanged between TON and Axxela — including DD checklists, Gantt charts, and the draft PPA — with Makir systematically excluded from every one.
- **June 27:** Draft PPA sent to TON only. NRG Bloom excluded from the key document. Nana Umebese later confirmed to Makir via WhatsApp (June 28) that she sent the PPA to David Billay.
- **July 3:** Nana Umebese re-includes Makir and requests both company profiles for Axxela Board approval.
- **July 4, 2025:** Marley Broda emails Franklin Umole (Axxela MD): **"After further discussions with Makir(NRG Bloom) and our team(TON), I want to confirm that the primary agreement for this project will be directly between Axella and TON. NRG Bloom is fully aware of this arrangement."** This statement was **provably false**. Makir had been excluded from PPA correspondence for 15 days and 15 emails, and had not agreed to this structure.

**Axxela's own response confirms NRG Bloom's centrality:**

- **July 7, 2025:** Franklin Umole (Axxela MD) emailed Makir directly: "Dear Makir, this is disappointedly coming very late in the game. We have made significant commitments on this basis of **our long discussions and agreement.**" Franklin addressed Makir — not Tyler, not Marley — because Franklin considered NRG Bloom his counterparty.
- **July 7, 2025:** Nana Umebese separately forwarded the PPA to Makir — recognising that NRG Bloom had been improperly excluded.
- **July 9, 2025:** Makir's detailed rebuttal documented NRG Bloom's role: "NRG Bloom led the due diligence process with Axxela, conducted the initial site exploration, developed the relationship with stakeholders, and initiated commercial negotiations, including successfully negotiating the $0.03/kWh power rate with Nana."

**September 2025:** Tyler confirmed to Makir and Julie that he was about to sign the PPA with Axxela executives.

TON offered NRG Bloom a $1,000/MW finder's fee ($10,000 total) for a relationship NRG Bloom originated, developed over five months, and negotiated to final terms. This offer is equivalent to **0.065% of the projected deal value**.

**NRG Bloom's origination and development role is established beyond dispute by Axxela's own records, emails, and meeting minutes.** The circumvention is documented email-by-email. Marley's false representation to Axxela is documented in writing. NRG Bloom's immediate objection is documented in writing with lawyer's approval.

**The JV Agreement's Section 5 exclusivity clause independently prohibits TON's pursuit of any Nigerian Bitcoin mining project without NRG Bloom.** TON's engagement with Axxela constitutes a direct breach of this commitment, actionable under Nigerian jurisdiction as part of the unified commercial arrangement between the parties.
