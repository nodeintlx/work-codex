## 3. FIVE LEGAL THEORIES (FROM ALBERTA LITIGATION ANALYSIS)

### Theory 1: Breach of Contract
- Breach of MNDA non-circumvention (Axxela exclusion)
- Breach of SDA termination provisions (zero-day notice vs. 30-day requirement)
- Breach of JV exclusivity clause (if not superseded)
- **Strength**: STRONG — documentary evidence is iron-clad

### Theory 2: Breach of Duty of Honest Performance (*Bhasin v. Hrynew*, 2014 SCC 71)
- Tyler's "calculated tactic" termination as negotiation leverage
- Marley's false July 4 email to Axxela: "NRG Bloom is fully aware"
- Progressive devaluation through repeated threats
- Manufactured termination (refused ops fee, then terminated for non-operation)
- **Strength**: STRONG — *Bhasin* originated in Alberta; Alberta courts are receptive

### Theory 3: Breach of Fiduciary Duty
- JV-like relationship creates fiduciary obligations
- TON exploited confidential information (Axxela contacts, gas pricing, site intelligence)
- Remedy: **disgorgement of ALL profits** (*Strother v. 3464920 Canada*, 2007 SCC)
- **Strength**: MODERATE — requires establishing fiduciary relationship existed

### Theory 4: Breach of Confidence (*LAC Minerals v. International Corona*, 1989 SCC)
- NRG Bloom shared confidential information in circumstances of confidence
- TON used that information to its own advantage (direct Axxela engagement)
- Remedy: constructive trust over the Axxela opportunity
- **Strength**: STRONG — direct parallel to *LAC Minerals* facts

### Theory 5: Tortious Interference with Economic Relations
- Marley's false email interfered with NRG Bloom's Axxela relationship
- TON's conduct destroyed NRG Bloom's business expectancy
- **Strength**: MODERATE — elements are present but tort claims in Alberta are narrower
