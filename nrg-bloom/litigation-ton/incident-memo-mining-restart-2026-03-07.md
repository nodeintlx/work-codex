# Incident Memo
# NRG Bloom Inc. v. TON Infrastructure Ltd.
# Reported Post-Termination Mining Restart and Loss of Pool Visibility
# March 7, 2026
# CONFIDENTIAL - PREPARED FOR COUNSEL

## Purpose

This memo summarizes a material post-termination development reported on March 7, 2026: TON appears to have restarted mining operations at the Ogboinbiri site on March 6, 2026, after terminating NRG Bloom and excluding NRG Bloom from the economic benefit and operational visibility of the site.

The purpose of this memo is to give counsel a clean incident record, identify what is directly observed versus inferred, and highlight the immediate evidentiary and strategic implications.

## Executive Summary

- On March 6, 2026, Makir observed a renewed hashrate spike on the NRG mining pool dashboard associated with machines at the site.
- Hours later, the dashboard visibility disappeared again.
- The most likely operational explanation is that the machines were initially still pointed at the NRG-configured pool, then were reconfigured by TON or the local crew to direct mining rewards elsewhere.
- Independent eyewitness and local-team reports state that the transformer was commissioned on March 6, 2026, which matches the timing of the observed hashrate activity.
- If confirmed, this is a major development: it indicates that TON terminated NRG Bloom, restarted the site, and then cut NRG Bloom out of both visibility and reward flow.

## Reported Facts

### 1. NRG pool dashboard activity on March 6, 2026

Makir had previously configured the relevant mining machines to point to the NRG mining pool.

On March 6, 2026, Makir observed through that pool dashboard that the site appeared to be mining again. The dashboard showed a spike in hashrate consistent with renewed machine activity.

Hours later, that visibility disappeared.

### 2. Loss of visibility after the initial spike

After the hashrate spike, Makir could no longer see ongoing mining activity or collect BTC rewards through the NRG pool setup.

### 3. Witness reports regarding site energization

Eyewitness accounts and local-team reports indicate that the transformer at the site was commissioned on March 6, 2026.

That reported commissioning date aligns with the timing of the observed hashrate spike on the pool dashboard.

## Observations vs. Inferences

### Direct observations

- Makir observed a hashrate spike on the NRG pool dashboard on March 6, 2026.
- The dashboard visibility then disappeared hours later.
- Local reports state that the transformer was commissioned on March 6, 2026.

### Reasonable operational inferences

- The hashrate spike indicates that the site had power and internet connectivity at that time.
- Because the machines had been configured to mine to the NRG pool, the temporary reappearance is consistent with the machines initially coming online under the prior configuration.
- The sudden disappearance from NRG's dashboard is consistent with TON or its local crew changing pool settings, wallet addresses, or both after startup.
- If that occurred, TON would have redirected mining output and BTC rewards away from NRG Bloom while continuing operations on infrastructure and relationships developed by NRG Bloom.

### What is not yet confirmed

- The exact wallet address currently receiving rewards.
- The exact pool now being used.
- The full amount of BTC mined since restart.
- The identity of the specific person who changed the machine configuration.

## Why This Matters

This development materially changes the posture of the dispute.

Before this event, TON could argue that alleged losses were contingent, future-facing, or speculative. After this event, the case becomes much stronger on active appropriation:

- TON terminated NRG Bloom.
- The site then appears to have been energized and restarted.
- Mining activity became visible through the NRG configuration.
- NRG visibility then ceased, consistent with a deliberate switch of pool or wallet settings.

That sequence supports a live narrative of exclusion, takeover, and diversion of mining proceeds, not merely a breakdown in negotiations or an unrealized business opportunity.

## Legal Relevance

Subject to counsel's review, this development appears relevant to:

- bad-faith termination and appropriation
- unjust enrichment
- conversion or diversion of economic benefit
- damages quantification
- urgency of evidence preservation
- urgency of any protective filing strategy

It also undermines any defense that the site remained non-operational or that NRG Bloom's loss theory is purely speculative.
