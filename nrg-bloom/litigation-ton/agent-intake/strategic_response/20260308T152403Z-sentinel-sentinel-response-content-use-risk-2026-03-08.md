## 3. COPYRIGHT vs. CONFIDENTIALITY ANALYSIS

### Legal Entitlement (what Makir likely owns)

- Under Canada's **Copyright Act, s. 13(1)**: the author is the first owner of copyright, absent employment or written assignment
- Makir was never employed by TON — he was an independent contractor / JV partner
- Neither the MNDA, JVA, nor SDA contain a copyright assignment clause (Sentinel has reviewed all three agreements)
- No written assignment exists → **Makir is presumptively the copyright owner of content he authored**
- Moral rights (attribution, integrity) belong to Makir and have not been waived

### BUT — Copyright Ownership ≠ Right to Publish

This is the critical distinction. Makir may own the copyright AND still be unable to publish because:

1. **MNDA Section 3 (Confidentiality)**: Covers "any and all confidential or proprietary information" exchanged between the parties. Content showing TON's operations, equipment, site layout, or business strategy likely falls within this definition. The MNDA is operative until January 10, 2027 (2-year term from signing). Publishing confidential information = breach of MNDA = undermines our own MNDA-based claims.

2. **Duty of confidence (equitable)**: Even beyond the MNDA's contractual scope, information received in confidence retains protection under the equitable doctrine (*LAC Minerals Ltd. v. International Corona Resources Ltd.* [1989] 2 SCR 574). We are RELYING on this doctrine for our breach of confidence claim against TON. Publishing confidential information would weaken our standing to argue the same duty against them.

3. **Mediation confidentiality**: Content created during or for the mediation process is protected. Nigerian Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023, Part III imposes confidentiality obligations on parties to mediation.

4. **Litigation privilege**: Content prepared in anticipation of litigation is privileged. Publishing it may waive privilege over related materials.

### Aegis should factor this as:
> **Copyright = Makir's. Right to publish = constrained by MNDA confidentiality, equitable duty of confidence, mediation privilege, and litigation risk. Copyright ownership is necessary but not sufficient for safe publication.**

## 4. SAFE INTERIM LANE

Yes, there is a safe lane. It is narrower than Makir might want, but it exists:

### 4A — Founder-Story Content (SAFE with care)

Makir can publish content about his **founder journey** that:
- Describes the experience of building data center infrastructure in West Africa **generically**
- References "working with international partners" without naming TON
- Discusses technical challenges (power, logistics, customs, community relations) **without site-identifying details**
- Frames NRG Bloom's capability and track record **without reference to the dispute**

**Test**: Would a reader be able to identify TON, the Ogboinbiri site, or the dispute from this content? If yes → HOLD. If no → safe.

### 4B — Generic Portfolio Material (SAFE with redaction)

Makir can use:
- Photos he personally took IF they show **only generic infrastructure** (containers, solar panels, generators) with no TON logos, serial numbers, or site-identifying backgrounds
- Technical drawings or specifications **he personally created** with TON identifiers removed
- General capability statements about containerized data centers, power systems, deployment methodology

**Redaction standard**: Remove all text, logos, metadata, and visual elements that could identify TON, the Ogboinbiri community, or specific counterparties.

### 4C — Private Evidence-Only Use (ALWAYS SAFE)

All content — regardless of category — can be:
- Shared with Dayo (counsel)
- Used to prepare evidence packages
- Referenced in legal filings and witness statements
- Preserved in Sentinel and Aegis case files

This is the primary legitimate use during proceedings.

### 4D — Coldstorm AI / New Business Content (FULLY SAFE)

Content created for Coldstorm AI, Oando engagement, or new business development has zero TON connection and can be published freely.
