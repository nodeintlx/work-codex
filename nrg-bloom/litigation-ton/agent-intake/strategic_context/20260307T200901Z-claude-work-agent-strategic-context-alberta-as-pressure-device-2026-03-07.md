## THE REFRAME: FILE IN ALBERTA, FIGHT IN NIGERIA

### Core Principle
**Alberta is not a battlefield. Alberta is a pressure device.**

The Alberta filing is a $2,350-$4,350 one-time expenditure that forces TON to spend $50,000-$150,000 responding. NRG Bloom never needs to take the Alberta case to trial. The case exists to generate pressure, not to be litigated.

### Two-Front Architecture

| Front | Role | Counsel | Cost Profile | Claims |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nigeria** | Primary battlefield | Dayo Adu (Moroom Africa) | Agreed pricing for mediation + arbitration | SDA claims: bad-faith termination, revenue share, operations fee, unjust enrichment |
| **Alberta** | Pressure artillery | Limited-scope Alberta lawyer (file + serve only) | $2,350-$4,350 one-time | MNDA claims: non-circumvention breach, breach of confidence, fraudulent misrepresentation, Tyler personal liability, punitive damages |

### What the Alberta Filing Does (Without Litigating)

1. **Stops the limitation clock** — preserves MNDA claims permanently (earliest deadline: July 11, 2027)
2. **Forces TON to hire and pay Alberta counsel** — Gary Zimmermann (McLennan Ross) bills from day one of service
3. **Opens discovery exposure** — mining revenues, Axxela deal terms, internal communications become discoverable under oath
4. **Names Tyler MacPherson personally** — his personal assets are now at risk, not just TON's corporate assets
5. **Creates a settlement concession chip** — in any deal, NRG Bloom offers to discontinue the Alberta action as a concession worth $50K-$150K in saved legal costs to TON
6. **Validates multi-jurisdictional threat** — Dayo's March 5 rejection letter referenced "multi-jurisdictional proceedings." The Alberta filing makes that credible, not just rhetoric.

### Cost Asymmetry — In NRG Bloom's Favor

| NRG Bloom Spends | TON Must Spend | Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| $2,350-$4,350 (one-time) | $50,000-$150,000 (ongoing) | **1:12 to 1:35** |

NRG Bloom controls the cost. The filing is a fixed cost. TON's costs are variable and ongoing — every motion, every status update, every report to their board about a second lawsuit costs money.

### What NRG Bloom Does NOT Need to Do in Alberta

- Does NOT need to conduct discoveries ($75K-$150K saved)
- Does NOT need to retain expert witnesses ($30K-$100K saved)
- Does NOT need to attend pre-trial motions ($50K-$100K saved)
- Does NOT need to go to trial ($100K-$250K saved)
- The case sits there. TON bleeds. Nigeria is where the resolution happens.

## WHY THE MNDA CLAUSE MAKES THIS BULLETPROOF

The MNDA (January 10, 2025) Section 6 states:

> "Any claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement or its subject matter or any right or obligation created by this Agreement, irrespective of legal theory or claims underlying such dispute, will be brought and determined exclusively in courts in the Province of Alberta."

This means:

1. **TON cannot get the Alberta case dismissed** — the MNDA's exclusive jurisdiction clause is the parties' own agreement. A court will enforce it.
2. **TON cannot send the MNDA claims to Nigerian arbitration** — the SDA's arbitration clause covers "this Agreement" (the SDA), not the MNDA. The MNDA has its own separate forum selection.
3. **The strongest claims are MNDA claims** — circumvention (Section 8), breach of confidence (Sections 1-2), fraudulent misrepresentation (relates to MNDA subject matter). These are the high-value, high-evidence claims.
4. **TON's only move is a partial stay** — they can try to send SDA-specific claims to Nigerian arbitration, but that's where NRG Bloom WANTS those claims anyway (Dayo handles them at Nigerian rates).

## INTERACTION WITH NIGERIAN TRACK

### What Dayo Needs to Know

The Alberta filing is not a competing proceeding — it's a coordinated pressure amplifier. Dayo should factor it into Nigerian negotiation posture:

- **Before Alberta filing:** TON faces one front (Nigeria). Cost to resist: whatever Nigerian mediation/arbitration costs.
- **After Alberta filing:** TON faces two fronts (Nigeria + Alberta). Cost to resist: Nigerian costs PLUS $50K-$150K Alberta defense costs PLUS Tyler's personal exposure PLUS discovery risk on mining revenues.

The Alberta filing compresses TON's settlement calculus. Every dollar they spend defending in Alberta is a dollar that could have gone to settling in Nigeria.

### Coordination Rules

1. **Nigeria leads the negotiation** — Dayo drives settlement discussions, mediator engagement, and arbitration preparation
2. **Alberta supports with pressure** — the filing exists as background leverage; referenced in negotiation but not actively litigated
3. **No conflict between tracks** — MNDA claims in Alberta, SDA claims in Nigeria. Different agreements, different forums, different claims. No risk of inconsistent judgments.
4. **Settlement resolves both** — any settlement agreement includes mutual release of all claims in all jurisdictions plus discontinuance of the Alberta action

### The March 5 Multi-Jurisdictional Reference

Dayo's rejection letter (March 5, 2026) obliquely referenced multi-jurisdictional proceedings. The Alberta filing transforms this from a threat into a reality. When TON's counsel sees the Statement of Claim served on their registered office, they know the multi-jurisdictional threat was not a bluff.
