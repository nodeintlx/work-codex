## BOTTOM LINE: THE ALBERTA PATH IS STRONGER THAN WE THOUGHT

The previous analysis assumed the SDA's arbitration clause was the primary risk to an Alberta filing. **That assumption was wrong.** The MNDA — which is the agreement containing the non-circumvention obligations and the breach of confidence protections — has an **exclusive Alberta court jurisdiction clause with no arbitration requirement.** The strongest claims in this case arise under the MNDA, not the SDA. TON cannot invoke a mandatory arbitration stay against MNDA claims.

## PART 1: THE TWO CLAUSES — VERBATIM

### SDA Dispute Resolution (labeled "6. Dispute Resolution" — second Section 6, drafting error)

> "Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be resolved through a first attempt of good-faith negotiations for thirty (30) days. If unresolved through negotiation, the dispute shall be submitted to mediation. If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within forty-five (45) days, **it shall be submitted to arbitration in accordance with the laws of the federal Republic of Nigeria.**"

**Key features:**
- Scope: "arising out of or in connection with **this Agreement**" — the SDA only
- Three-step escalation: negotiation (30 days) → mediation (45 days) → arbitration
- Seat of arbitration law: Nigeria
- No named arbitral institution (ad hoc arbitration)
- No specification of number of arbitrators, language, or procedural rules

### MNDA Governing Law and Jurisdiction (Section 6)

> "This Agreement will be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Province of Alberta. **Any claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement or its subject matter or any right or obligation created by this Agreement, irrespective of legal theory or claims underlying such dispute, will be brought and determined exclusively in courts in the Province of Alberta.** Each of the Parties to this Agreement hereby irrevocably submits to the jurisdiction of courts in the Province of Alberta and agrees not to challenge jurisdiction or choice of venue for this Agreement, its subject matter, or any right, obligation, or dispute arising hereunder. The Parties agree that this Agreement is performable in Alberta."

**Key features:**
- Governing law: Alberta
- Forum: **Exclusive** Alberta court jurisdiction — "will be brought and determined exclusively in courts"
- Scope: Extraordinarily broad — "any claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement **or its subject matter** or any right or obligation created by this Agreement"
- Theory-neutral: "**irrespective of legal theory or claims underlying such dispute**"
- Irrevocable submission: parties "irrevocably submit" and agree "not to challenge jurisdiction or choice of venue"
- No arbitration clause whatsoever
- Expressly contemplates court proceedings (Section 4 — Injunctive Relief: "entitled to seek injunctive and other equitable relief")

A tort claim about misrepresenting a party's awareness of dealings with an MNDA-introduced contact falls squarely within this. |
| **Duty of honest performance (re: MNDA)** | Bhasin duty attaches to the MNDA as a contract | TON's dishonest conduct in performing (and undermining) the MNDA's non-circumvention obligations triggers the Bhasin/Callow duty. |

### Claims that are INDEPENDENT of both agreements → Alberta Court

| Claim | Basis | Why It Escapes Both Clauses |
|---|---|---|
| **Tyler MacPherson personal liability** | Independent tortious conduct | Tyler is not a party to the SDA (signed by NRG Bloom and TON). The MNDA was signed by Marley Broda for TON. Tyler's personal tortious acts are independently actionable. |

| **Punitive damages** | Whiten v. Pilot | Attaches to the independent actionable wrongs, not to any single contract. |

### The JVA Exclusivity Clause (Section 5)

The JVA is a third agreement. Its Section 5 states: "TON agrees that all current and future Bitcoin mining projects in Nigeria will be done exclusively with NRG BLOOM." The JVA's own dispute resolution mechanism (if any) would govern. If the JVA has no dispute resolution clause, the MNDA's Alberta exclusive jurisdiction clause likely applies as the foundational agreement governing the relationship. **This needs to be confirmed by reading the JVA.**

## PART 3: THE MANDATORY STAY ANALYSIS — CLAIM BY CLAIM

Under Alberta's International Commercial Arbitration Act (RSA 2000, c I-5), which incorporates the UNCITRAL Model Law, Article 8 requires a court to refer parties to arbitration if:

1. A valid arbitration agreement exists
2. The claims fall within its scope
3. The agreement is not null and void, inoperative, or incapable of being performed

**TON's likely motion:** Apply to stay all Alberta proceedings under Article 8, arguing the SDA's arbitration clause governs the entire dispute.

**NRG Bloom's response — claim by claim:**

| Claim | Stay Applies? | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| MNDA non-circumvention breach | **NO** | Arises under the MNDA, which has its own exclusive Alberta court clause. The SDA arbitration clause only covers "this Agreement" (the SDA). The MNDA is a separate, prior agreement with its own forum selection. |
| Breach of confidence (MNDA) | **NO** | Same — confidential information was disclosed and protected under the MNDA. The breach of confidence claim relates to MNDA subject matter. |
| Fraudulent misrepresentation | **NO** | Tort claim. The MNDA's "irrespective of legal theory" language captures it. Even without the MNDA clause, tort claims are generally not subject to contractual arbitration clauses unless the clause is very broadly worded AND the tort is inseparable from the contract. |
| Duty of honest performance (MNDA) | **NO** | Bhasin duty attaches to the MNDA. Alberta courts, not Nigerian arbitrators, should determine this under Alberta law. |
| Tyler personal liability | **NO** | Tyler is not a signatory to the SDA. Non-signatories cannot be compelled to arbitrate under Canadian law except in narrow circumstances (group of companies doctrine — rarely applied in Canada). |
| SDA revenue/fee claims | **YES — likely stayed** | These arise under the SDA and fall within the arbitration clause. |
| SDA termination claims | **YES — likely stayed** | Same — the termination was an exercise of SDA rights. |
| Unjust enrichment | **ARGUABLE** | Not a contractual claim, but TON will argue it's "in connection with" the SDA. Courts split on this. Strong argument it's independent. |
| Punitive damages | **NO** | Attaches to non-stayed claims. If the underlying claim survives the stay, punitive damages survive too. |

**Net result of a stay application:** TON gets the SDA-specific claims (revenue, fees, termination) sent to Nigerian arbitration. NRG Bloom KEEPS the strongest claims in Alberta court — non-circumvention, breach of confidence, fraudulent misrepresentation, Tyler's personal liability, and punitive damages. This is actually the better outcome for NRG Bloom because:

1. The MNDA claims are the high-value claims (Axxela circumvention = $15.3M deal value)
2. The SDA claims are the lower-value claims (revenue share, operations fees)
3. Alberta court provides full discovery — Nigerian ad hoc arbitration may not
4. An Alberta court judgment against an Alberta company is directly enforceable

## PART 4: CORRECTING PREVIOUS ANALYSIS ERRORS

### Error 1: "The dispute resolution clause says 'legal proceedings,' NOT 'arbitration'"

The McCarthy call brief stated: "The clause says 'legal proceedings,' NOT 'arbitration.' This is significant — it may mean court litigation is the default, not arbitration."

**CORRECTION:** The SDA's dispute resolution clause DOES say "arbitration" — verbatim: "it shall be submitted to arbitration in accordance with the laws of the federal Republic of Nigeria." The McCarthy brief summary was wrong. This IS an arbitration clause.

### Error 2: Treating the SDA arbitration clause as the primary risk to the entire Alberta strategy

**CORRECTION:** The SDA arbitration clause only covers "this Agreement" — the SDA. The MNDA has a SEPARATE exclusive Alberta court jurisdiction clause. The strongest claims (non-circumvention, breach of confidence) arise under the MNDA and are immune to a stay application. The Alberta strategy is stronger, not weaker, than previously assessed.

### Error 3: Not distinguishing between MNDA claims and SDA claims

**CORRECTION:** The two agreements have fundamentally different dispute resolution mechanisms. The litigation strategy must be structured to emphasize MNDA claims (Alberta court, no arbitration) while accepting that SDA claims may be referred to Nigerian arbitration. This is a feature, not a bug — the MNDA claims are the high-value claims.

## PART 5: THE MNDA NON-CIRCUMVENTION CLAUSE — VERBATIM (SECTION 8)

> "The Parties agree that each Party shall be entitled to compensation for its introduction and consulting purposes in regard to disclosing information, location and introduction to third parties (each, a 'Referred Party'). Compensation shall be negotiated through a subsequent agreement between the Parties for the introduction and identification of a Referred Party. **Each Party agrees that it will not enter into an agreement for the introduced property or Referral Party without first entering into a prior written agreement with the other Party relating to its compensation for services. The Parties shall not in any way whatsoever circumvent or attempt to circumvent each other's rights pursuant to this Agreement.**"

**Why this is devastating for TON:**

1. "shall not in any way whatsoever circumvent or attempt to circumvent" — absolute prohibition, no qualifications
2. "each other's rights pursuant to this Agreement" — the MNDA's rights include the right to compensation for introductions (Axxela)
3. "will not enter into an agreement for the introduced property or Referral Party without first entering into a prior written agreement with the other Party" — TON entered direct arrangements with Axxela (NRG Bloom's introduced contact) without any prior written compensation agreement with NRG Bloom
4. This clause is in the MNDA, which has exclusive Alberta court jurisdiction — there is no arbitration escape route

## PART 6: ADDITIONAL MNDA PROVISIONS SUPPORTING THE ALBERTA CLAIM

### Section 4 — Injunctive Relief

> "The Recipient acknowledges that there is a substantial likelihood that the breach of this Agreement by the Recipient or any of its Representatives would cause irreparable injury to the Disclosing Party, and that there is a substantial likelihood that money damages would be an inadequate remedy... the Disclosing Party... will be entitled to seek injunctive and other equitable relief to enforce the provisions of this Agreement."

**Significance:** The MNDA itself contemplates court enforcement via injunctive relief. This is inconsistent with arbitration — parties agreed that breaches of the MNDA would be enforced through courts, not arbitrators. This reinforces the Alberta court path.

### Section 9 — Termination (2-Year Duration)

> "The Parties' respective duties and obligations under this Agreement will terminate two (2) years after the Effective Date."

Effective Date: January 10, 2025. Termination: **January 10, 2027.**

The Axxela circumvention occurred June-July 2025 — well within the MNDA's operative period. The MNDA was fully enforceable when every act of circumvention took place.

### Signature Page — Discussion Scope

> "The Parties hereby desire to set forth their agreement and understanding with respect to any Confidential Information disclosed by either Party during the course of **general business discussions, property related discussions and crypto currency mining site location services.**"

**Significance:** The MNDA's scope explicitly covers "crypto currency mining site location services" — which is exactly what the Axxela/Ogboinbiri relationship involved. TON cannot argue the Axxela circumvention falls outside the MNDA's scope.

## PART 7: STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR THE ALBERTA FILING

### 7.1 Restructure the Statement of Claim

The current draft Statement of Claim leads with all 7 causes of action without distinguishing MNDA claims from SDA claims. **It should be restructured to lead with MNDA claims.** This ensures that even if TON successfully stays the SDA claims, the core of the case proceeds in Alberta.

Recommended pleading order:
1. **Breach of MNDA non-circumvention (Section 8)** — primary claim, exclusive Alberta jurisdiction
2. **Breach of confidence (MNDA Sections 1-2 + LAC Minerals)** — MNDA subject matter, Alberta jurisdiction
3. **Fraudulent misrepresentation** — tort, MNDA "irrespective of legal theory" clause
4. **Breach of duty of honest performance (MNDA)** — Bhasin/Callow, Alberta law
5. **Tyler MacPherson personal liability** — non-signatory, independent torts
6. **Breach of JVA exclusivity (Section 5)** — separate agreement, forum TBD (check JVA)
7. **Breach of SDA terms** — acknowledge this may be subject to stay; plead in the alternative
8. **Unjust enrichment** — independent restitutionary claim
9. **Punitive damages** — attaches to surviving claims

### 7.2 Prepare for the Stay Application

TON's counsel (Gary Zimmermann, McLennan Ross) WILL bring a stay motion under the International Commercial Arbitration Act. NRG Bloom's response:

**Argument 1 — Severability:** The MNDA and SDA are separate agreements with separate dispute resolution mechanisms. The SDA's arbitration clause cannot override the MNDA's exclusive court clause. Canadian courts respect party autonomy — the parties agreed to different forums for different agreements.

**Argument 2 — Scope limitation:** The SDA arbitration clause covers "this Agreement" only. MNDA claims are not "arising out of or in connection with" the SDA.

**Argument 3 — The MNDA's "irrespective of legal theory" language:** Even if TON recharacterizes the claims as SDA claims, the MNDA's jurisdiction clause captures claims "irrespective of legal theory or claims underlying such dispute." This anti-avoidance language was designed to prevent exactly what TON will attempt.

**Argument 4 — Injunctive relief carve-out:** The MNDA (Section 4) expressly contemplates court-based injunctive relief. Courts routinely hold that injunctive relief provisions in contracts are inconsistent with mandatory arbitration.

**Argument 5 — Non-signatory (Tyler):** Tyler MacPherson cannot be compelled to arbitrate. His claims must proceed in court regardless. Bifurcating the action (some claims to arbitration, Tyler's to court) is inefficient — the court should retain jurisdiction over all related claims.

**Argument 6 — Inoperability:** The SDA's arbitration clause specifies no institution, no procedural rules, no number of arbitrators, no seat, and no language. It simply says "arbitration in accordance with the laws of the federal Republic of Nigeria." An argument can be made that this clause is too vague to be operative — "inoperative or incapable of being performed" under Article 8(1) of the Model Law. Nigerian arbitration law (Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023) requires specificity that this clause lacks.

### 7.3 The Two-Front Advantage

If TON gets a partial stay, NRG Bloom is BETTER positioned:
- **Alberta court:** Non-circumvention, breach of confidence, fraud, Tyler personal liability, punitive damages — the high-value claims with full discovery
- **Nigerian arbitration:** Revenue share, operations fee, termination — the lower-value claims that Dayo Adu handles

This gives NRG Bloom two fronts, two jurisdictions, and maximum litigation cost pressure on TON. TON has to defend in both places simultaneously.
