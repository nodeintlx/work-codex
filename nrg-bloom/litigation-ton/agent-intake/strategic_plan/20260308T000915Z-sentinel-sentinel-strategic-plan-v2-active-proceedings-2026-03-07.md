- **The held-back evidence is tailored for impeachment.** WhatsApp messages and call recordings break the case when deployed against live testimony under oath.
- **The MNDA exclusive jurisdiction clause.** Unknown to TON. Makes the Alberta filing bulletproof.
- **Dayo is executing.** He isn't waiting — service of process is happening this week.

### Weaknesses (the truth)
- **Budget.** NRG Bloom cannot afford prolonged multi-track litigation. This is the single biggest constraint.
- **Axxela quantum is arguable.** PPA was never executed. $264K/month is a projection from TON's own model, but an arbitrator may discount significantly.
- **SDA Section 5 is a real defense.** Not a winner for TON, but it has weight — especially on Ogboinbiri claims.
- **McCarthy is paused.** If TON discovers this, the litigation threat loses credibility. Alberta filing must replace it as the credibility signal.
- **Self-representation gap.** In Nigeria, Dayo covers it. In Alberta, a lawyer is needed for the corporate entity.
- **Damages quantification is the weakest area.** Needs work — see Section below.

### What TON is counting on
TON's strategy is **delay + cost pressure + lowball = NRG Bloom folds.** Their $10K offer against a $600K+ claim was a bet, not a negotiation. They're betting NRG Bloom runs out of resources before enforcement. Dayo's letter just told them that bet is going to be expensive.
