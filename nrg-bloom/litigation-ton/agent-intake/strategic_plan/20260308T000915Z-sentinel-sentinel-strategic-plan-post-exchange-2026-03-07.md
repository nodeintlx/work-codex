### B.1 — Arbitration Framework (CRITICAL — Dayo must decide)

The SDA says "arbitration in accordance with the laws of the federal Republic of Nigeria" but specifies nothing else. Under the Nigerian Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023, the following must be determined:

| Question | Options | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Institutional or ad hoc? | Institutional (LCA, ICC Lagos, LCIA) vs. ad hoc under Nigerian law | Dayo advises — institutional is more predictable but more expensive; ad hoc is cheaper but less structured |
| Rules? | LCA Rules, ICC Rules, UNCITRAL Rules, or AMA 2023 default | UNCITRAL Rules are neutral, widely accepted, and cheaper than ICC |
| Number of arbitrators? | 1 (cheaper, faster) or 3 (more authority, more expensive) | 1 arbitrator — keeps costs down. NRG Bloom proposes this; if TON insists on 3, costs are split |
| Seat? | Lagos, Abuja, or other Nigerian city | Lagos — Axxela is there, Dayo is there, most arbitration practitioners are there |
| Language? | English | English (both parties are English-speaking) |
| Governing law (substantive)? | Nigerian law | Nigerian law — per SDA and per the parties' commercial relationship |

### B.5 — Key Legal Issues for Dayo

These are the questions Dayo needs to address for arbitration, with Sentinel's preliminary analysis:

**1. SDA Section 5 discontinuation defense**
- TON's argument: Section 5 allows discontinuation after any phase with no damages beyond existing revenue sharing
- Sentinel's analysis: Weak because (a) terminated mid-phase, not after completion, (b) clause was absent from JVA — inserted in SDA under duress, (c) doesn't cover Axxela (separate opportunity), (d) unconscionable if it allows TON to consume 13 months of work and pay nothing
- **Dayo needs to confirm**: What is Nigerian arbitral practice on unconscionability? Is there a "fundamental breach" doctrine that overrides contractual limitation clauses?

**2. Affirmation defense**
- TON's argument: NRG Bloom performed for 13 months without rescinding, therefore affirmed the SDA
- Sentinel's analysis: Mitigated by (a) 6+ documented objections, (b) no practical alternative after surrendering Marshall, (c) legal counsel engaged from July 2025, (d) continuous duress — threats never stopped. Even if affirmation bars the duress claim, it does NOT bar circumvention, unjust enrichment, or misrepresentation
- **Dayo needs to confirm**: Under Nigerian law, does ongoing performance under continuous duress constitute affirmation?

**3. Axxela damages methodology**
- The quantum is the arbitration's center of gravity. Dayo needs to decide: do we claim (a) lost share of projected revenue ($264K/month × NRG Bloom %), (b) quantum meruit for origination (8.5–10% of deal value per *Energy Venture Partners*), (c) both?
- **Dayo needs to confirm**: What methodology will a Nigerian arbitrator accept? Is expert evidence standard or unusual?

**4. Is the arbitration clause even enforceable?**
- SDA says "arbitration in accordance with the laws of the federal Republic of Nigeria" but names no institution, no rules, no number of arbitrators, no seat, no language
- Under the Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023, this may be "incapable of being performed" — a ground for refusing to enforce
- **Dayo needs to confirm**: Is this clause specific enough to be operative? If not, can NRG Bloom argue for court proceedings instead?
