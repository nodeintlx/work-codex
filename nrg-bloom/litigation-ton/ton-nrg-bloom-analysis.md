## 4. POWER DYNAMICS

### Who Was Driving the Partnership?

**NRG Bloom (Makir and Julie) were clearly driving the partnership.** Evidence:
- Makir organized and financed the Nigeria trip infrastructure
- Makir flew to Edmonton (May 2025) at personal expense to save the relationship
- Makir repeatedly initiated follow-ups, sent updates, pushed timelines
- NRG Bloom managed all Nigerian ground operations
- NRG Bloom introduced every Nigerian contact and relationship (Agnes, Axxela, Mikkydon)

TON's role was primarily as capital/equipment provider. They shipped containers and ASICs but had no independent operational capability in Nigeria.

### Who Was Doing the Work?

**NRG Bloom handled the vast majority of operational work:**
- All Nigerian logistics (customs, transport, security, warehousing)
- Community relations and PPA negotiations
- Visa facilitation and trip coordination
- Technical site measurements and specifications
- Following up on unanswered TON communications

**TON's contributions were primarily:**
- Container and ASIC procurement (from their Canadian operations)
- Container design (David Bailey Billay)
- Shipping logistics from Canada (Brian Claypool)
- Financial payments (often delayed)

### Who Was Making Decisions?

**Decisions were asymmetric:**
- Operational decisions on the ground: Made by NRG Bloom (Makir, Julie, Ola initially)
- Financial decisions (when to pay, how much): Made by TON (Marley), often with delays
- Strategic decisions (partnership continuation): Made by TON unilaterally. Tyler was the one who could threaten termination (May 2025), and ultimately TON terminated on Feb 12, 2026 without negotiation visible in this chat.

The power imbalance is clear: NRG Bloom did the work and bore the risk, while TON held the purse strings and the power to walk away at any time.
