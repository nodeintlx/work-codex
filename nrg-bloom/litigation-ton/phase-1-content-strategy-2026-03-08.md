# Phase 1 Content Strategy
## Safe Audience-Building Plan During Active TON Proceedings

Created: 2026-03-08
Purpose: build Makir's public credibility, brand, audience, and narrative leverage without harming the TON litigation or exposing the workspace's litigation intelligence.

This is the pre-disclosure strategy.

The goal is not to tell the TON story yet.
The goal is to build the audience that will later care when the TON story can be told safely.

Sentinel review confirms the safe lane is narrower than instinct suggests:

- if a reader could identify TON, the Ogboinbiri site, or the dispute from the content, it is not Phase 1 safe
- generic founder-story content is the right lane
- held content has more current value as evidence than as social posts

## Strategic Objective

Phase 1 should do four things at once:

1. establish Makir as a credible builder/operator
2. grow reach and trust around his real-world execution experience
3. create a public archive of lessons that does not prejudice the case
4. reserve the TON-specific story for the right legal and strategic moment

## Positioning

Public identity for Phase 1:

- diaspora builder
- operator in hard environments
- infrastructure storyteller
- Nigeria execution insider
- founder who has learned expensive lessons about capital, trust, and operations

Do not position Makir yet as:

- litigant-in-public
- whistleblower on TON
- public accuser
- aggrieved former partner telling the case before filings are safely public

Do not let the Phase 1 brand become:

- "the guy publicly fighting his former partner"
- "the person using media to pressure active proceedings"

## Phase 1 Message

The safest useful message is:

"I built and operated real infrastructure under difficult conditions, learned hard lessons about execution, trust, capital, and local reality, and I am sharing those lessons for other builders."

That is strong enough to build audience and safe enough to avoid fighting the case on social media.

Test for every Phase 1 post:

"Could a reasonable reader connect this post to TON, the site, or the active dispute?"

If yes, the post is not Phase 1 safe.

## What To Avoid In Phase 1

Avoid these topics for now:

- naming TON
- identifying the site
- naming community actors or counterparties
- discussing the legal dispute directly
- describing mediation, arbitration, or litigation strategy
- posting site-identifiable footage
- showing contracts, emails, letters, or chat screenshots
- attributing motive to the other side
- saying anyone "stole" anything
- using any visual or detail that indirectly identifies the Ogboinbiri site
- publishing any financial or commercial figures tied to the dispute
- using WhatsApp, email, or call-derived material as story fuel

Use for:

- one lesson per episode
- no live dispute framing

Tone:

Use for:
- hooks

- lesson snapshots

Best format:
- 20 to 60 seconds
- one idea
- strong opening sentence

Tone:
- memorable
- controlled
- not ranting

## Recommended Publishing Rhythm

Minimum viable cadence:

- 2 LinkedIn posts per week
- 3 to 5 X posts or short threads per week
- 1 short-form video per week
- 1 long-form video every 2 weeks

This is enough to build narrative presence without overextending.

## Four-Lane Posting Rule

Before publishing, classify the source material:

- `hold`: do not post
- `reserve_for_counsel`: send only to Dayo / use for evidence
- `redact_and_repurpose`: edit into generic founder/portfolio content
- `publish`: safe as-is

For Phase 1, almost all TON-adjacent material will begin in `hold` or `reserve_for_counsel`.

## Safe Publishing Checklist

Before posting, confirm:

- no TON name
- no identifiable site
- no legal correspondence
- no mediation content
- no active dispute framing
- no accusation of intentional wrongdoing
- no confidential numbers or operational details from the live matter
- no contradiction with the litigation narrative

If any of those fail, do not post.

Additional red flags:

- would the post give TON advance notice of evidence or themes?
- would TON's counsel plausibly call it harassment, retaliation, or trial by media?
- would the post undermine an MNDA confidentiality position we may rely on?

If yes, do not post.

## Escalation Trigger For Phase 2

Phase 2 should start only when one of these is true:

- Dayo says the case posture is public-safe
- public filings make the dispute safely discussable
- TON goes public first and requires a controlled response

Until then:

Phase 1 builds the audience.
Phase 2 deploys the story.
