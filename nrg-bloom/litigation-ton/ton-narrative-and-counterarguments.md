## TON's Internal Narrative

TON's version of events goes something like this:

*"We're a serious Canadian mining infrastructure company that took a chance on an ambitious but under-resourced operator in Nigeria. We brought the capital — millions in containers, miners, logistics. Makir brought local contacts and hustle, but no money. We gave him a fair deal for what he contributed. Over time, the situation on the ground became chaotic — disputes with Nelson, disputes with Ola, community unrest, power shutdowns, staff couldn't get paid. NRG Bloom couldn't hold it together. We exercised our contractual right to terminate because the site was a mess and NRG Bloom had lost its authority there. Now Makir is trying to extract millions based on inflated valuations of introductions and 'sweat equity.' We followed the contract. We owe him nothing beyond what the contract says."*

## The 7 Pillars of TON's Defense

### Pillar 1: "The Contract Is King"

**Their argument**: The May 16, 2025 Site Development Agreement governs everything. Section 9 gives unconditional 30-day termination. Section 5 says TON can discontinue after any phase without breach. NRG Bloom signed it. End of story.

**What they'll say in mediation**: "We followed the letter of the agreement. We gave proper notice. There's no breach here. If NRG Bloom didn't like the terms, they shouldn't have signed."

**What this really is**: Their entire defense rests on the contract being a sealed box — they want the mediator to look ONLY at the contract text and ignore everything around it. If they can keep the conversation inside the four corners of the agreement, they think they win.

**How to kill it**:
- The contract was signed under duress — Tyler's May 12 termination threat came the night before Makir's non-refundable $2,600 flight. Sign or lose everything.
- Even lawful termination doesn't immunize bad faith conduct. You can have the right to end a contract AND still be liable for HOW and WHY you ended it.
- Section 9 doesn't grant the right to appropriate the other party's relationships, contacts, and work product after termination.
- The MNDA (signed earlier) has non-circumvention provisions that SURVIVE termination — the contract isn't the only document.
- **Kill shot**: Jeff on the Feb 12 tape — "We're clearing the house and bringing our guys in." The termination wasn't about operational concerns. It was a takeover. The contract was the weapon, not the reason.

### Pillar 2: "We Did All the Real Work on Axxela"

**Their argument**: NRG Bloom made one introduction call. TON built the financial models, negotiated the PPA terms, managed the due diligence, and committed the $5M+ capital. The Axxela deal belongs to TON.

**What they'll say in mediation**: "Makir introduced us to Franklin. That's it. Everything after that — the spreadsheets, the negotiations, the site planning, the capital — was TON. A phone call isn't worth millions."

**What this really is**: They need to minimize NRG Bloom's role to justify the $1,000/MW finder's fee and their unilateral decision to sign with Axxela directly. If NRG Bloom is just an "intro," they owe nothing meaningful.

**How to kill it**:
- 58 emails between NRG Bloom and Axxela (Feb 4–May 24) with ZERO TON involvement. That's not "one phone call" — that's 4 months of relationship building.
- Makir personally met Axxela at their Lagos offices (Feb 25 meeting minutes exist).
- Makir negotiated the $0.03/kWh rate through direct conversations with Nana Umebese.
- Tyler's first appearance in ANY Axxela email was May 25 — introduced BY Makir.
- Marley ON TAPE (Jul 4): "You did find the client and find the project, the opportunity, right? There's no doubt about that." — Their own COO admits it.
- **Kill shot**: Marley's claim on the same tape that "TON has done all the legwork" is directly contradicted by 58 emails. Their financial model (David Billay's spreadsheet) was built on a deal NRG Bloom originated and nurtured for 4 months. The model has value only because the relationship exists.

### Pillar 3: "NRG Bloom Was the Problem — Site Was a Disaster"

**Their argument**: The Ogboinbiri site descended into chaos. Nelson disputes, Ola disputes, community anger, power shutdowns, safety violations, unpaid staff. NRG Bloom couldn't manage the situation. TON had no choice but to terminate and take direct control.

**What they'll say in mediation**: "We invested millions. The site was falling apart. NRG Bloom couldn't pay its own people. There were serious safety violations. We had to act to protect our investment."

**What this really is**: The strongest emotional play they have. They want the mediator to see them as the responsible party trying to save a chaotic situation, and NRG Bloom as the chaotic element that caused the mess.

**How to kill it**:
- **The chaos was caused by TON's own actions**:
  - Agnes shut off power on Dec 30 because TON wasn't communicating (Agnes's Dec 23 complaint blames TON directly)
  - Tyler ghosted for 55 days (Oct 18–Dec 22) — NRG Bloom couldn't get answers
  - Marley refused the operations fee on Dec 4 — BEFORE any site issues: "Management fee - not sure this site needs to get online"
  - NRG Bloom was paying security, maintaining the site, protecting TON's equipment — all unpaid
- **The "safety violations" are post-hoc**:
  - On the June 24 call, David Billay himself proposed upgrading the container's electrical. TON KNEW the specs and was planning upgrades. You can't claim to "discover" problems you'd been planning to fix for 6 months.
- **The timeline proves it**:
  - TON refused ops fee (Dec 4) → NRG Bloom ran out of money → Staff couldn't get paid → Site deteriorated → TON used the deterioration to justify termination
  - TON manufactured the crisis, then blamed NRG Bloom for it
- **Kill shot**: Julie on the Feb 12 tape: "we lost our revenue in our container for you guys. And so we had to make everything work with minimal, minimal revenue to keep the site safe for you guys as well." NRG Bloom was subsidizing TON's operations. The "site chaos" was a direct result of TON starving NRG Bloom of operating funds.

### Pillar 4: "NRG Bloom Agreed to Every Deal — No Duress"

**Their argument**: Every successive agreement was "executed voluntarily" (§3.1.3). NRG Bloom performed under the contract for 13 months without formally objecting. Under Nigerian law, that's "affirmation" — you accepted the terms. You can't come back now and claim duress.

**What they'll say in mediation**: "Nobody forced Makir to sign anything. He agreed to the 8%. He agreed to the operations structure. He performed happily for over a year. Now that it didn't work out, he wants to rewrite history."

**What this really is**: Their second-strongest legal argument (after the contract itself). If they can establish that NRG Bloom voluntarily accepted each reduction, it undercuts the "progressive devaluation" narrative.

**How to kill it**:
- The duress was continuous and documented:
  - Tyler's Apr 21 termination threat when Makir pushed back on Obrikom
  - Tyler's May 12 termination notice — the night before Makir's non-refundable flight
  - Jeff on Jul 1 call: "if you're not willing to split it, my recommendation would be walking" — threats to abandon NRG's container [T4-I]
  - Marley Jul 22: "If the offer isn't accepted by NRG then we disagree on that opportunity and move on" — take it or lose everything
- NRG Bloom DID object — repeatedly:
  - Apr 11: Makir raises circumvention concern
  - Jul 18: Makir's detailed economic objection ($1K/MW = 0.065%)
  - Dec 24: Formal complaint about communication breakdown
- The "voluntary" agreements were signed under an implicit threat: accept or we walk, and you lose everything you've built
- **Kill shot**: Julie on the Jul 1 tape documents the exact pattern: "the first deal was 250W miners and $9,000/month... and then from then on, we just went down... all the way to 8%." She then explains WHY they accepted: "we didn't have a choice. We took the deal that Marshall offered us because at least we could showcase that our project was working." It was never voluntary — it was survival.

### Pillar 5: "Jurisdiction Splitting — Make It Too Expensive to Fight"

**Their argument**: The MNDA (Alberta law) governs non-circumvention. The Agreement (Nigerian law) governs operations. NRG Bloom has to file in TWO jurisdictions. Double the lawyers, double the cost, double the time.

**What they're really doing**: This isn't a legal argument — it's a war of attrition. They know NRG Bloom is a small company. They're betting Makir can't afford to fight on two fronts and will settle cheap.

**How to kill it**:
- In mediation, jurisdiction doesn't matter — the mediator hears everything.
- The circumvention evidence is relevant CONTEXT for the Agreement dispute regardless of which court technically handles the MNDA claim.
- Dayo should argue: the same conduct (circumvention, misrepresentation, bad faith) gives rise to claims under BOTH documents — you can't firewall the facts.
- **Kill shot**: Don't let them split the conversation. In the position paper and mediation, present the FULL narrative. The mediator will see the Axxela circumvention and the Ogboinbiri termination as one connected pattern. Procedural splitting only works in formal litigation — not in mediation.

### Pillar 6: "Counterclaims — We Can Hurt You Too"

**Their argument**: Reserved 6 counterclaims including misappropriation of community funds, security breach, safety violations, misrepresentations.

**What they're really doing**: These aren't real claims. Not one is quantified. Not one cites documentation. They're leverage — designed to make Makir think "if I push too hard, they'll countersue." Classic defense lawyer tactic: create fear of a counter-punch.

**How to kill it**:

- "Misappropriation of community funds" — No evidence offered. This is defamatory if unsupported.

- **Kill shot**: Every counterclaim dissolves when you ask: "If NRG Bloom was so terrible, why did TON invest millions relying on them? Why did Jeff say on July 4 'no headaches, no problems'? Why didn't TON raise any of these concerns until AFTER NRG Bloom sent a demand letter?"

## Summary: Strongest vs. Weakest

**Their strongest argument**: The contract gives unconditional termination rights, and NRG Bloom signed voluntarily. If the mediator looks ONLY at the contract text, TON has a defensible position.

**Their weakest point**: They have NO answer for the Axxela circumvention. No answer for Marley's July 4 email. No answer for the call transcripts. They completely ignore the strongest evidence because they can't explain it.

**Their strategy**: Keep it narrow (contract only), keep it procedural (jurisdiction), keep it expensive (two fronts), and keep it intimidating (counterclaims).

**Our strategy**: Blow it wide open. The position paper should make it impossible for the mediator to look at just the contract. The pattern — origination, devaluation, circumvention, misrepresentation, manufactured crisis, termination, appropriation — is one continuous story. Once the mediator sees the whole picture, the contract arguments become what they are: the legal dress on a bad-faith takeover.
