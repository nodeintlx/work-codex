# TON Infrastructure Ltd. -- Litigation Context

## Who is TON and What Happened

TON Infrastructure Ltd. is a Canadian Bitcoin mining infrastructure company founded by Tyler MacPherson and led by CEO Marley Broda, with Jeff Jespersen as VP of Operations and Brian Claypool as Project Manager. They operate mining sites in Alberta, Canada, and entered a partnership with NRG Bloom Inc. in February 2025 to deploy mining infrastructure at the Ogboinbiri site in Bayelsa State, Nigeria -- a site, community, and opportunity that NRG Bloom had originated, developed, and introduced to TON in its entirety.

The relationship began in February 2025 when Makir Volcy (NRG Bloom's CEO) organized TON's first trip to Nigeria, arranging visas, flights, armed security escorts, a 7-day itinerary, and introductions to every critical stakeholder: Agnes (community Power of Attorney), the Community King, Axxela (gas/power provider with a $15.3M five-year opportunity), Nelson/Shugamine, and local officials. Tyler MacPherson pledged "I give you my word I will do everything in my power to help you better yourself and the communities." A Joint Venture Agreement was signed on February 19, 2025, initially providing NRG Bloom a 1MW allocation, all mining rewards from that allocation, and a monthly operations fee of USD $9,000.

From the moment the partnership was formed, TON engaged in a pattern of progressive devaluation through repeated termination threats. In April 2025, after NRG Bloom objected to TON's circumvention of the Obrikom site opportunity (which NRG Bloom had introduced), the initial terms were reduced to a 40% revenue split. Then, on May 12, 2025, Tyler sent a termination notice via WhatsApp at approximately 4:42 AM -- the night before Makir and Julie Peeters had confirmed, non-refundable flights to Edmonton. Makir flew anyway, spent 10 days at Marley's home saving the partnership, and three days after arriving, the parties signed a new agreement on May 16, 2025 with terms further devalued to an 8% revenue share and USD $875/month operations fee. Tyler later admitted to Makir, in Julie's presence, that the termination had been a "calculated tactic." In July 2025, Marley emailed Axxela's Managing Director Franklin Umole stating "NRG Bloom is fully aware" that the deal would be directly between Axxela and TON -- a statement that was false. NRG Bloom had never agreed to this arrangement, and Makir immediately objected and invoked the MNDA's non-circumvention provisions.

For thirteen months, NRG Bloom managed all of TON's Nigerian operations: customs clearance for two shipping containers, transport from Lagos to the remote Ogboinbiri site, land lease payments (paid out of pocket when TON delayed), community relations (25% revenue to the community), security (2 guards and a site manager), Starlink internet, fencing, site preparation, and regulatory compliance. NRG Bloom cleared a pre-existing client (Marshall Long) to secure exclusive access for TON, mitigated a serious threat from Nelson (who threatened border denial), mediated the Ola crisis that endangered TON's container, and managed every ground-level logistics challenge from power outages to crane repairs to barge scheduling. The revenue split ended at 67% TON / 33% NRG Bloom, with NRG Bloom managing distribution to the community (20%), itself (8%), and Nelson (5%).

Despite NRG Bloom's 13 months of continuous service, TON paid NRG Bloom exactly nothing in operations fees and nothing in revenue share for the 40ft container deployment. NRG Bloom's only compensation was a 33% hashrate allocation on 30 ASIC miners for approximately five weeks (December 22, 2025 to January 26, 2026) -- less than 1% of the value NRG Bloom created and transferred to TON.

In December 2025, after 10 months of knowing about NRG Bloom's 20ft container infrastructure, TON suddenly raised "urgent" electrical safety concerns and imposed unilateral deadlines over the Christmas holiday period. When the deadlines weren't met (in rural Nigeria, during holidays), TON marked them "OVERDUE" and began building a termination case. Critically, Agnes -- the community Power of Attorney -- filed a formal written complaint on December 23 documenting TON's communication failures: "I must be honest -- I am not comfortable with a lack of response or failure to pass information." Seven days later, Agnes ordered a power shutdown in direct response to TON's non-responsiveness. Marley's last message in the community coordination group was November 19 -- over a month of silence during the operational crisis she was supposed to be managing. Meanwhile, TON refused to pay the contractual operations fee (Marley on December 4: "not sure this site needs to get online"), directly causing the staff payment delays they would later cite as termination grounds.

On February 9, 2026, Jeff Jespersen responded to Makir's comprehensive operational plan with: "We're going to take this away, digest it, and get back to you on how we will navigate our next steps." Three days later, on February 12, 2026, TON terminated the partnership with zero warning, citing "security concerns" and NRG Bloom being "no longer authorized to operate" -- arguments that were factually incorrect and based on conditions TON itself had created by refusing to pay the operations fee and failing to communicate with community stakeholders.

NRG Bloom's lawyer, Dayo Adu of Moroom Africa, issued a 20-page Letter of Demand on February 16, 2026 invoking Section 7 dispute resolution and claiming damages of ₦1.55B--₦4B ($939K--$2.4M USD). TON responded on February 20 with a 7-page letter categorically rejecting all claims, raising jurisdictional challenges, asserting unconditional termination rights, and reserving counterclaims -- but notably failing to deny key facts including the Axxela circumvention, the "calculated tactic" admission, the progressive devaluation, and NRG Bloom's foundational role. TON agreed to a 30-day good faith negotiation period, which is currently active.

## Core Pattern: Extract, Exploit, Terminate

TON's conduct follows a clear pattern:
1. **Promise** -- Make grand commitments to secure NRG Bloom's cooperation
2. **Extract** -- Use NRG Bloom's relationships, local knowledge, and labor to enter the Nigerian market
3. **Devalue** -- Threaten termination to renegotiate terms downward after NRG Bloom has invested
4. **Circumvent** -- Go directly to introduced parties (Axxela, Agnes) behind NRG Bloom's back
5. **Starve** -- Refuse to pay agreed fees, creating financial pressure on NRG Bloom
6. **Blame** -- Cite the resulting instability as justification for termination
7. **Terminate** -- Walk away with all value at the precise moment revenue was about to flow

- **NRG Bloom's position:** Termination was wrongful; claims for unjust enrichment, breach of contract, circumvention, economic duress; seeking ₦1B--₦2B settlement
- **TON's position:** Termination was lawful under Section 9; all claims rejected; counterclaims reserved; MNDA claims must go to Alberta
- **Key deadline:** ~March 18, 2026 (end of 30-day negotiation period)
- **If negotiation fails:** 45-day mediation, then arbitration under Nigerian law
