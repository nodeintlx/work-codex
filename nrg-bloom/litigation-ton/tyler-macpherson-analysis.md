### Relationship Arc
The chat reveals a relationship that moved through five distinct phases:

1. **Enthusiasm and Courtship (Feb 12 -- Feb 28, 2025):** Tyler was effusive, grateful, and eager. He traveled to Nigeria, signed a JV agreement, and made sweeping promises about commitment.
2. **Active Collaboration (Mar -- Apr 2025):** Regular calls, conference attendance in Miami, technical discussions about power requirements and Axxela partnership. Tyler was engaged but increasingly hard to reach.
3. **Deterioration and First Termination Attempt (Apr 21 -- May 12, 2025):** Tyler became evasive, Makir expressed frustration at being ghosted, and Tyler issued a termination notice on May 12, 2025 -- the night before Makir was flying to Alberta to work with TON.
4. **Rehabilitation and Reset (May 13 -- Jun 2025):** Despite the termination, Makir traveled to Alberta anyway, spent over a week working at the barn/office, and the relationship was patched. Operations continued.
5. **Slow Death and Ghosting (Jul -- Dec 2025):** A nearly 3-month gap (Jul 4 -- Sep 21), then sporadic exchanges. Tyler became almost entirely unreachable. The last message in the chat is Makir attempting group calls on December 22, 2025 with no response logged from Tyler.

### Tone Summary
- **Tyler's tone:** Oscillated between warmth/gratitude and cold/dismissive. When things were good, he called Makir "sir" and "boss," made grand promises, and expressed excitement. When challenged or when accountability was due, he became condescending (lecturing Makir about "emotions"), evasive, and unavailable.
- **Makir's tone:** Consistently professional, persistent, and earnest. He repeatedly expressed enthusiasm, followed up diligently, and showed a desire to make the partnership work. Even when frustrated, he articulated his concerns respectfully.

## 4. TYLER'S CHARACTER PROFILE

### Communication Style
Tyler communicates primarily through voice notes and calls rather than written messages. His written messages frequently contain spelling and grammatical errors (*"witch"* for "which," *"hioneslty"* for "honestly," *"reacdh"* for "reach"), which is consistent with someone who dictates rather than types. He is often difficult to reach and frequently unavailable: the chat contains 15+ instances of unanswered calls from Makir, and Tyler's standard responses are *"Just on a call,"* *"I am offline till this afternoon,"* *"Just at the doctors,"* and *"Can I call you later?"*

### Professionalism Assessment
- **Positive:** Tyler was initially warm, respectful, and showed genuine enthusiasm for the partnership. He traveled to Nigeria, transferred money when needed on the ground, and participated in important calls (e.g., the Axxela call on Apr 5).
- **Negative:** Tyler's professionalism deteriorated significantly over time:
  - He made promises with deadlines and consistently failed to deliver.
  - He became condescending when held accountable.
  - He terminated the partnership the night before a pre-approved visit.
  - He ghosted his JV partner for months at a time.
  - He redirected accountability to subordinates (Marley, Brian, Dave) rather than owning decisions as a principal of TON.

### Respect for Partnership Terms
Tyler did not respect the partnership terms. Key failures:
1. The JV Agreement was signed Feb 19, 2025, but Tyler never returned a signed copy (Makir asked Apr 7).
2. Decisions about the Obrikom site were communicated to third parties but not to the JV partner.
3. The management fee structure was never resolved despite being flagged as critical.
4. The May 12 termination was sent via WhatsApp, not through proper contractual channels, and the promised "official" termination email the next day appears to have been the start of a protracted process rather than a clean break.
5. After the Alberta rehabilitation, Tyler once again became unreachable, breaking his own promise to always pick up Makir's calls.

### Evidence of Going Behind NRG Bloom's Back
- **Communications with Nelson/Shugamine about declining Obrikom** without informing Makir.
- **Brian's parallel communications** on costing and execution that Makir was unaware of.
- **The deleted message on May 12** (line 409) suggests Tyler composed and then retracted content he did not want preserved.
- **The July-September gap** overlaps with the period when Ola's interference was escalating at the Ogboinbiri site (per CONTEXT.md). It is notable that TON went silent during the exact period when NRG Bloom was dealing with its most serious operational crisis.

### Pattern Summary
Tyler MacPherson presents as someone who was initially charmed by the opportunity and made enthusiastic commitments he could not or would not keep. When the reality of execution proved complex, he disengaged incrementally. When held accountable, he became defensive and condescending. When it came time to terminate, he did so with poor timing and a patronizing tone. His behavior pattern -- promise, delay, deflect, ghost, blame the other party's "emotions" -- is consistent across the entire relationship arc.
