## 7. Call Strategy — Proposal-Led Conversation

### The Core Principle
**They have the proposal. Don't re-pitch it. Let them react to it.**

The worst thing NRG Bloom can do is spend 30 minutes presenting what Oando has already
read. The best thing NRG Bloom can do is spend 5 minutes setting context, then say:
"What questions came up when your team reviewed the proposal?"

This positions Makir as a peer, not a salesman. It shows confidence in the document.
And it immediately tells you what Oando cares about — which is intel you need.

### Recommended Call Flow (60 minutes)

**Phase 1: Context Setting (10 min) — Makir leads**

Open with gratitude and context. Do NOT re-read the proposal. Instead, anchor on
three things the proposal alone can't convey:

1. **We're already there.** "As you saw in the proposal, we've been operating at
   Ogboinbiri since February 2025. We're not a company asking you to take a chance
   on an idea — we've been running on your infrastructure for over a year, generating
   revenue, employing local staff, and navigating the Niger Delta operating environment
   daily. This is about formalizing and scaling what's already working."

2. **We wrote the proposal to give you options.** "We structured three pathways because
   we don't presume to know which fits Oando's position on community obligations and
   gas allocation. We're flexible on structure. We're committed on outcome."

3. **The pivot to them.** "We'd love to hear your team's initial read — what stood out,
   what questions came up, and which direction feels right for Oando."

**Phase 2: Oando's Questions (35 min) — Listen, then respond**

This is the core of the call. Let Oando drive. Expect questions to cluster around:

- **Pathway selection:** "We're more interested in the gas arrangement" (Pathway 3) —
  this is the most likely direction given that Chioma routed to Gas Business
- **Ogboinbiri specifics:** How is the current arrangement working? What's the community
  relationship? How much power are you actually using?
- **Technical / gas specs:** What gas volumes do you need? What equipment? (Section 4.1)
- **Commercial terms:** Are those rates negotiable? How does the revenue share work?
- **Scale / next sites:** You mention OMLs 60–63 in the proposal — how does that work
  with the NNPC JV?
- **Credibility check:** Who else are you working with? What's your track record?

5. **Close with confidence.** "We're already on the ground, the proof of concept is done.
