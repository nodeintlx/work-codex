## Executive Summary

Oando is Nigeria's largest indigenous oil and gas company. Following its landmark $783M acquisition of Nigerian Agip Oil Company (NAOC) in August 2024, it now controls 40 oil and gas fields, 1,490 km of pipelines, and has committed to eliminating routine gas flaring by 2027. The NAOC acquisition shifted their reserve mix to 61% gas. They are under regulatory and ESG pressure on flaring, and their MD of the upstream entity (Dr. Ainojie Irune) holds a PhD in Computer Science and previously ran Oando's clean energy subsidiary.

**The NRG Bloom opportunity is real, well-timed, and well-aligned.** Oando needs to monetize the gas they cannot yet eliminate. NRG Bloom can deploy the infrastructure; Oando provides gas access and the ESG narrative. The NGFCP regulatory framework provides a legal pathway for third-party flare gas developers at Oando's sites.

**Key risk:** Historical SEC governance controversy (2017–2019), negative operating cash flow in FY2024 (-₦545.5B), and NNPC JV complexity for the biggest assets (OMLs 60–63). Recommend starting with OML 56 or OML 13 (Oando-controlled, no JV complications) for a pilot.

## Section 7: Strategic Analysis

### 7.1 Oando's Motivations

- **Regulatory compliance (strongest):** 2027 Zero Flare commitment. Failure = penalties + license risk.
- **Revenue from wasted asset:** Flared gas = zero revenue + fines. Any offtake turns liability into income.
- **ESG profile (JSE listing):** South African institutional investors require ESG disclosure.
- **Zero capex needed:** Oando is capital-constrained. NRG Bloom deploys; Oando provides gas and site access.

### 7.2 Likely Objections & Responses

| Objection | NRG Bloom Response |
|---|---|
| "We have our own Zero Flare Programme" | "We accelerate it and monetize what you'd otherwise eliminate at cost." |
| "We don't understand data centers" | "You don't need to — we operate it. Revenue share + ESG credit for you." |
| "Security at remote Delta sites" | "Containerized, hardened, relocatable. We handle operations." |
| "NNPC JV approval needed for OMLs 60–63" | "Let's pilot on OML 56 or OML 13 first where you have autonomous control." |
| "Who else has done this in Nigeria?" | "Green Flare (53MW, Delta State), Aggreko/MTN (5MW gas DC), your own Kwale-Okpai (960MW gas-to-power)." |

### 7.3 Decision-Making Path

1. **First contact:** Chioma Anyamele (OER) or Ololade Shenbanjo (PLC BD)
2. **Technical/commercial evaluation:** Dr. Ainojie Irune (MD, OERNL)
3. **Final approval:** Wale Tinubu (GCEO)
4. **JV complication (OMLs 60–63 only):** NNPC concurrence — 12–18 month timeline

### 7.4 Red Flags

- **SEC governance controversy (2017–2019):** Investigated for false disclosures, market abuse. Resolved but reputational.
- **Negative operating cash flow (-₦545.5B, FY2024):** Capital-constrained. Zero-capex for Oando is essential.
- **NNPC JV dependency (OMLs 60–63):** Don't plan as Phase 1.
- **Niger Delta security:** Pipeline vandalism explicitly cited as risk. Start with most secure sites.
- **Green Flare competition:** Active in Delta State with 53MW. Window is open but moving.
- **Stock correction:** Down 39% from 2024 peak. May signal cost-cutting pressure.
