## 1. EXPORT DEVELOPMENT CANADA (EDC)

### A. Working Capital & Export Guarantees

**Trade Expansion Lending Program (TELP)**
- Fastest, simplest option — EDC shares risk with your bank, bank gives you more credit
- Delivered through your Canadian financial institution
- Use for: cash flow, pre-deployment costs, working capital for Nigeria ops
- No stated minimum; designed for SMEs
- How to apply: Through your bank — ask about TELP

**Export Guarantee Program (EGP)**
- Maximum total coverage: **USD $25 million**
- Use for: operating lines, term loans, international investments, equipment

### B. Direct Lending (Mid-Market)
- Minimum revenue: **$10 million CAD**
- Loan minimum: **$1 million**
- Note: If current revenue is below $10M, focus on TELP/EGP first

### C. Structured & Project Finance
- Project cost minimum: **CAD $50 million**
- Expected EBITDA minimum: **CAD $10 million**
- Medium-term target once Oando partnership is formalized as infrastructure SPV

### D. Political Risk Insurance (PRI)
- Coverage: Up to **90% of losses** from political violence, expropriation, currency inconvertibility, transfer restrictions
- Nigeria is fully eligible (NOT on exclusion list)
- Can improve project credit rating from B- to BBB — dramatically reduces financing costs

### E. Clean Tech / Green Finance
- **$12 billion+** provided to 440+ cleantech companies (exceeded 2025 goal 2 years early)
- 500 cleantech companies supported in 2024 alone
- Net Zero commitment by 2050 — first export credit agency globally
- NRG Bloom's flare-gas-to-power model is a strong fit for EDC's cleantech mandate

### F. Nigeria Appetite
- EDC published specific article on West Africa infrastructure gap (2025)
- EDC notes Nigeria "produces less power than Wyoming" — frames as Canadian opportunity
- Explicitly calls out: grid stabilization, storage, smart-grid, digital infrastructure

- **Phone: 1-800-229-0575** (9am–5pm ET)
- Website: edc.ca
- Ask for: Trade Advisor for Sub-Saharan Africa
- Timeline: TELP 2–4 weeks; PRI 4–8 weeks; Project finance 6–18 months

## 3. CANEXPORT SMEs — APPLY NOW

- **Funds:** 50% of eligible export development costs, up to **$50,000 CAD per project**
- **Eligible costs:** Travel to Nigeria, trade shows, marketing materials, website adaptation, video content
- **Eligibility:** Canadian incorporated, 1–500 employees, $100K–$100M revenue (2025-26 cycle; rising to $300K in 2026-27)
- **Submit:** At least 60 business days before first activity
- **Deadline:** May 29, 2026 at 12:00 PM ET (2025-26 fiscal year)
- **Approval rate:** ~40% of applicants
- **Portal:** tradecommissioner.gc.ca

**This could fund your China trip and Nigeria travel.**
