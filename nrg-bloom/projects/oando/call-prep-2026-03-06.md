## Call Flow (60 min)

### Phase 1: Context Setting (10 min) — Makir

**Do NOT re-present the proposal.** They've read it. Eleas was specifically assigned to evaluate it.

Open with three things the proposal alone can't convey:

1. **"We're already there."**
   "As you saw in the proposal, we've been operational at Ogboinbiri since February 2025 — over a year. This isn't a concept. We've been running on existing infrastructure in the Niger Delta daily."

2. **"We wrote three pathways so you can choose."**
   "We structured the proposal with three options because we don't presume to know which fits Oando's operational position. We're flexible on structure, committed on outcome."

3. **Pivot to them:**
   "We'd love to hear your team's initial read — what stood out, what questions came up, and which direction feels right for Oando."

Then **stop talking and let them drive**.

### Phase 3: Steer and Close (15 min) — Makir

1. **Confirm pathway direction:** "Based on this conversation, it sounds like [Pathway X] is the right starting point. Does that match your team's view?"

2. **Identify what they need:** "What does your internal process look like from here? What information do you need from us?"

3. **Request technical data:** If Pathway 3: "To finalize equipment specs and confirm gas pricing, we'll need gas chromatographic data and flow measurements. Can Eleas's team provide that?"

4. **Propose follow-up:** "Can we schedule a follow-up technical session in the next two weeks? Given this would sit under OERNL operationally, would it make sense to include Dr. Irune or his designated technical lead?"

5. **Close with confidence:** "The proof of concept is done. The question is which structure works for Oando and how fast we move. We're ready."
