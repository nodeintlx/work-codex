## 5. EVIDENCE OF WRONGDOING BY OLA OLANIYAN

### 5.1 Unauthorized Interference with Operations
- Ola made "unauthorised communications with members of the local community, employees, and representatives such as Agnes" (Cease and Desist, Oct 27, 2025)
- Used associate **Yakubu (Tanimu Yakubu)** to "advance demands outside the terms of the executed Investment Agreement" (Cease and Desist, Oct 27, 2025)
- After receiving the first cease and desist, Ola confirmed his intention to contact the TON team -- NRG Bloom's key business partner -- in direct defiance of the cease and desist order (Final Cease and Desist, Nov 24, 2025)

### 5.2 Unauthorized Site Shutdown (January 2026)
- Ola's actions (or those of his agents) caused an **unauthorized shutdown at the Ogboinbiri site** (Incident Report, Jan 27, 2026)
- This constituted a "security breach that led to the site power shutdown" per TON Infrastructure (Jan 28, 2026)
- Ola's people physically placed a **lock on the container/transformer**, preventing NRG Bloom and TON from accessing their own equipment (Feb 9, 2026 email)

### 5.3 Corrupting Site Management
- **Blaze**, NRG Bloom's site manager, "stopped following my [Makir's] instructions and is actually blocking us from getting to TON's equipment" (Feb 9, 2026)
- Blaze "let Ola's people walk in and place the lock on the container/transformer" (Feb 9, 2026)
- NRG Bloom has "reasons to believe that Nelson and Ola are paying people in the community to challenge Agnes' authority" (Feb 9, 2026)

### 5.4 Destruction of Key Business Relationship
- Ola's interference directly led to **TON Infrastructure removing their equipment** from the site and halting operations (Jan 28, 2026)
- TON Infrastructure ultimately **terminated the Ogboinbiri 5MW Site Development & Revenue Sharing Agreement** (Feb 12, 2026)
- This was a catastrophic business loss for NRG Bloom, directly attributable to the instability Ola created

### 5.5 Refusal to Engage in Good Faith
- Ola ignored Makir's first settlement offer (Nov 1, 2025) offering full return of his USD investment
- Ola's response to the final cease and desist was simply: "I will see you in court!" (Nov 24, 2025)
- Ola sent "URGENT! Notice of removal" demands (Jan 23, 2026) instead of engaging in settlement discussions
- Ola took unilateral physical possession of equipment on NRG Bloom's financed land without authorization (per Feb 17, 2026 letter to the King)
- Makir's correspondence "has gone unanswered" (Feb 17, 2026)

### 5.6 Exceeding Authority Under Investment Agreement
- The Investment Agreement gave Ola only an 8% equity stake as a passive investor
- Ola was "not a director, officer, or agent of NRG Bloom Inc." with "no authority whatsoever to represent, direct, or communicate on behalf of the Company"
- Despite this, Ola communicated with community members, employees, business partners (TON), and took physical actions at the site

### 5.7 Unlawful Occupation
- Ola "has taken possession of a container and transformer situated on land that I have personally financed and paid for" (Feb 17, 2026)
- Makir characterizes this as a "material breach and a direct disregard for the established legal position"

3. **Equipment inaccessibility:** Ola's agents physically locked NRG Bloom/TON equipment, preventing operations
4. **Cost of replacement infrastructure:** NRG Bloom forced to purchase new 40ft container from China
5. **Shugamine contract termination:** Additional contract (April 4, 2025) terminated

### Indirect Damages
1. **Management disruption:** Site manager Blaze was compromised, requiring replacement
2. **Community relations damage:** Ola and Nelson allegedly paying community members to undermine Agnes and NRG Bloom's authority
3. **Legal costs:** Multiple rounds of legal correspondence, cease and desist letters, settlement negotiations
4. **Reputational damage:** NRG Bloom's credibility with business partners undermined
5. **Lost revenue:** Bitcoin mining operations at Ogboinbiri site halted during the dispute

## 9. KEY LEGAL ARGUMENTS SUPPORTED BY THIS EVIDENCE

1. **Breach of Investment Agreement:** Ola exceeded the scope of his 8% passive equity investment by attempting to exercise executive authority, communicating with employees and partners, and physically interfering with operations.

2. **Tortious Interference with Business Relations:** Ola's direct contact with TON Infrastructure (NRG Bloom's key partner) after being explicitly told not to, contributing to the termination of the 5MW Revenue Sharing Agreement.

3. **Defiance of Legal Notice:** Ola received two formal cease and desist notices and responded to the second with "I will see you in court!" while continuing the prohibited conduct.

4. **Unauthorized Physical Interference:** Ola's agents placed locks on equipment, caused a site power shutdown, and corrupted the site manager -- all constituting trespass and conversion.

5. **Bad Faith Refusal to Settle:** Makir offered to return Ola's full USD 20,000 investment (Nov 1, 2025) and later offered Ola the physical assets (transformer + container) he claims to want (Feb 2, 2026). Ola ignored both offers.

6. **Community Disruption and Intimidation:** Evidence suggests Ola and Nelson paid community members to challenge NRG Bloom's local representative Agnes, constituting intimidation and interference.
