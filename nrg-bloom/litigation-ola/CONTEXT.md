# Oladipo Olaniyan ("Ola") -- Litigation Context

## Who is Ola and What Happened

Oladipo Olaniyan ("Ola") is a London-based Nigerian national who entered the NRG Bloom relationship in July 2024 as a prospective investor, presenting himself as a well-connected operator with deep Nigerian networks and technical expertise in IT infrastructure. He signed an Investment Agreement on August 5, 2024, committing USD 20,000 for an 8% passive equity stake with no executive or managerial authority -- though he only ever transferred $5,000 USDT directly, with the remainder attributed to transformer procurement costs he managed on the ground through his personal agent, Yakubu Tanimu. Ola knew from the outset that he was investing in NRG Bloom the company -- not in a specific site, not in a revenue stream, and not in a personal arrangement with Makir. He signed an agreement for 8% equity in a company under the leadership of its CEO.

In the early months, Ola was a genuine contributor: he identified inflated pricing from contractors, handled transformer logistics, attended industry conferences on NRG Bloom's behalf, facilitated introductions including a connection to a senior executive at NELMCO (Nigeria's electricity liability management agency), and helped negotiate customs clearance for imported equipment. He and Makir traveled to Nigeria together, attended the Africa Bitcoin Conference in Nairobi together, and built what appeared to be a real working partnership. But from the very beginning, Ola embedded himself not as a passive investor but as a parallel power centre -- he created the company's internal operations group chat, directed staff, compiled financial documents, gave orders to the CEO, and installed his own man (Yakubu) as the eyes, hands, and wallet on the ground, ensuring that Nigerian operations could not function without his involvement.

The fundamental disagreement that destroyed the relationship was about what the revenue was for. The Ogboinbiri site was a proof of concept running on poor-quality machines supplied by Marshall and Trojan -- who were effectively the same team -- and generating approximately $1,500 to $2,000 USD per month. It was never much. Makir, as CEO, directed that revenue back into company operations and the company budget: paying site staff, maintaining equipment, covering security, keeping the lights on, and building the foundation for NRG Bloom to grow beyond a single site into the scalable infrastructure company it was designed to be. Ola wanted that revenue sent directly to him. He wanted immediate ROI on his contributions, a personal cut of every dollar the site produced, regardless of what the company needed to survive and develop. Makir told him no -- the money was not enough to both sustain operations and pay out investors, and the entire point of Ola's investment was an equity stake in a growing company, not a weekly dividend from a proof-of-concept site barely covering its own costs. Ola rejected this. He did not care about NRG Bloom as a company anymore. He wanted the infrastructure Makir built, the site Makir funded, and the revenue the operation generated -- just without NRG Bloom, without the CEO's authority, and without the terms of the agreement he signed.

Once Ola fixated on extracting that revenue, he disregarded the contract entirely and began making his own rules. On July 6, 2025, he issued an ultimatum -- *"I'm pulling the plugs"* -- and ordered Yakubu to shut down the transformer that powered the entire site. Twenty days later, in the company's internal group chat with witnesses present, he repeated the threat more explicitly: *"@Tanimu Yakubu tell them to turn off the site Im done with this shit"* -- and Yakubu confirmed *"Ok, will do so."* From that point forward, Ola waged a sustained campaign to either extract money or destroy the business. His financial demands escalated from $150/week to $250/week to $300/week to a $50,000 lump sum within 30 days, none of which had any contractual basis. He called Makir a "grifter," questioned his LinkedIn credentials, told him *"if you can't cook get out the kitchen, my boy,"* publicly humiliated him in front of team members, attempted to turn Makir's own brother against him, demanded VPN and admin access to company networks, investigated Bitcoin wallet configurations to track revenue flows, and ultimately announced in the group chat on September 8, 2025 that *"Moving forward my wallet address will get the revenue and I wil send makir share to him"* -- an open declaration of intent to seize company income. When Makir's lawyer issued two cease and desist letters and Makir offered to return the full $20,000 investment in a settlement, Ola ignored both and responded *"I will see you in court."* In January 2026, Ola's agents physically locked the container and transformer, shutting down the site entirely -- an act that directly caused TON Infrastructure, NRG Bloom's most important strategic partner, to terminate their 5MW Revenue Sharing Agreement, and Shugamine to terminate their contract, destroying in weeks what Makir had spent two years and tens of thousands of dollars building. Ola did not destroy NRG Bloom because he was wronged. He destroyed it because he could not own it.
