The fundamental disagreement that destroyed the relationship was about what the site revenue was for. It was never much. As CEO, I directed that revenue back into company operations and the company budget: paying site staff, maintaining equipment, covering security, keeping the lights on, and building the foundation for NRG Bloom to grow beyond a single site into the scalable infrastructure company it was designed to be. Ola wanted that revenue sent directly to him. He wanted immediate ROI on his contributions, a personal cut of every dollar the site produced, regardless of what the company needed to survive and develop. I told him no -- the money was not enough to both sustain operations and pay out investors, and the entire point of his investment was an equity stake in a growing company, not a weekly dividend from a proof-of-concept site barely covering its own costs. Ola rejected this entirely. He did not care about NRG Bloom as a company anymore. He wanted the infrastructure I built, the site I funded, and the revenue the operation generated -- just without NRG Bloom, without my authority as CEO, and without the terms of the agreement he signed.

Once Ola fixated on extracting that revenue, he disregarded the contract entirely and began making his own rules. On July 6, 2025, he issued an ultimatum and ordered his agent Yakubu to shut down the transformer that powered the entire site. His financial demands escalated from $150/week to $250/week to $300/week to a $50,000 lump sum within 30 days -- demanding half or more of the site's total monthly output for himself alone. He publicly humiliated me in front of team members, attempted to turn my own brother against me, demanded VPN and admin access to company networks, investigated Bitcoin wallet configurations to track revenue flows, and on September 8, 2025 declared in the company group chat: *"Moving forward my wallet address will get the revenue and I wil send makir share to him."* He was no longer pretending to be an investor.
